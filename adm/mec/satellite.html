Gravitational Potential Energy

The potential gravitational energy reduces to U = mgh only for objects near the surface of the earth. Hoewever for a satellite of mass m in a circular orbit around the earth a more general expression must be used.

A conservative force is obtained as the negative gradient of a scalar potential energy U:

F = −∇U

The difference in gravitational potential energy of system between two points is given by

U 2 − U 1 = − ∫ r 1 r 2 F ⋅ d r

The definition of a potential energy entails a reference point r1 at which the potential energy is set to be zero — that is, an absolute potential energy does not exist. So let r1 = ∞ with U1 = 0, and r1 = r with U1 = U(r). Then

V ( r ) = − ∫ ∞ r F ⋅ d r

The gravitational potential energy increases from a large negative value to zero as the distance increases to infinity. If F is the gravitational force acting on a test body m located at a distance r from point mass M, we can write

U = − ∫ ∞ r G m n r 2 r ^ = − G M m r

This is the gravitational potential energy of a system of two point masses. As the distance r increases, the gravitational potential energy also increases as it becomes less negative.

Consider an artificial satellite of mass, m, revolving around earth. We admit that the satellite can be considered as a material point and that earth has spherical symmetry and mass M. The total energy K + U is conserved that is

E = ½ mv2 + (−GMm/r)

r increases, the gravitational potential energy also increases and K decreases, that is the satellite moves slower when is distant from earth and faster when it is close to it.

In the case of a circular orbit both the kinetic and potential energy remain constant. From Newton's 2nd law of dynamics ma = ΣF

mv2/r = GMm/r2

By multiplying both hand sides of this equation by ½r we obtain

½mv2 = (½GMm)/r2

The kinetic energy of the satellite is a half of the absolute value of gravitatioanl potential energy. The mechanical energy E = K + U is

E = (½GMm)/r

The negative sign is a consequence of the fact that the 0 is taken for an infinite distance between the masses. The mechanical energy of an orbiting artificial satellie is less than zero.
If we compare E which a free object (not subject to the gravitationa field) has since for r ⟶ ∞ U ⟶ 0 and K ≥ 0 we have that E = K + U ≥ 0. Thus an orbiting satellite has less energy than a free one. The fact the total energy is negative is characteristic of a bound system: the satelline is bound to the Earth and it cannot escape from it (without the injection of some energy from outside). The minimum energy that the satellite needs to "unbind" itslef from Earth is that which makes the total energy zero. The is is called binind energy and it is equal to |E|. Since E = 0, the Binding Energy is the energy required to remove the satellite from its orbit to infinity Uf = 0 with final kinetic energy as mimimum possible i.e. Kf = 0. If the satellite is initially at distance ri (Ui = −GMm/ri) from earth posseses escape velocity vi (Ki = ½ mv2) corresponding to its initial velocity. If Ei = ½ mv2 + (−GMm/ri) = 0. Thus

v i = 2 G M r i

is the escape velocity from the position ri. Notice how this velocity depends only on the earth mass M, but not the satellite m mass.
