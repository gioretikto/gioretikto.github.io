Fluids

Laminar means layered. In laminar flows, layers of fluid slide over one another with very little mixing between the layers.

In an arbitrary flow of fluid, the two mechanisms (convective and diffusive) for momentum transport are simultaneously active but, depending on the velocity and geoemtry of the flow, they will not have the same order of magnitude.

Reynolds Number

When a fluid flow through a certain path, a specific flow pattern forms depending on various factors. Reynold number is a special indicator that represents the flow pattern in a specific number.

A high level definition of Reynolds Number is described as follows. As you can see from this equation, Reynalds Number represents the ratio of inertial forces and viscous forces of a fluid. As inertial forces increases, Reynolds number descreases and as viscous forces increases, Reynolds number decreases.

Re = inertial forces/viscous forces

Reynolds number can be described in a form showing more specific properties of the fluid and physical dimension of the path the fluid flow through. Its value indicates whether a flow is likely to be laminar (direct or smooth) or turbulent (sinuous).

Re = ρuL/μ

  • ρ density of the fluid (kg/m3);

  • u, average velocity (m/s);

  • L, characteristic dimension of the path the fluid flow through; (e.g. diameter ofr a pipe for flow through a pipe);

  • μ Dynamic viscosity of the fluid (Pa or N⋅s/m2).

This expression is often written in terms of the kinematic viscosity ν = μ/ρ:

Re = uL/ν

The Reynolds number is low for laminar flows and high for turbulent flows, with transition flows at values in between. In a flow at low Reynolds number, viscous forces and the associated diffusice transport dominate. On the other hand, in the case, of flow at large Reynolds number, the transport of momentum by convection dominates, and appears in the form of non-linear terms containg products of the velocity components and their gradients.

Example. In una tubazione a sezione circolare scorre a temperatura ambiente ed in condizioni ideali acqua pura. Nel tratto iniziale il diametro della tubazione è d1 = 25 cm e il moto del liquido è caratterizzato da un numero di Reynolds Re1 = 40000. Quale sarà il valore del numero di Reynolds se nel tratto finale il diametro della tubazione risulta raddoppiato rispetto a quello iniziale?

Ans. La velocità nel secondo condotto si ottiene come:

v1 ⋅ π (25/2)2 = v2 ⋅ π (50/2)2
v2 = v1/4; la velocità diminuisce di 4 volte.

Il numero di Reynolds dipende dalla velocità e dal diametro del condotto:

Re1 = v1 ⋅ 25/ν
Re2 = (v1/4) ⋅ 50/ν

Allora Re2 = 20000.  ■

Prandtl Number

Convection heat transfer occurs due to fluid movement.

The Prandtl number is the ratio of momentum diffusivity to thermal diffusivity and is defined the ratio of the kinematic viscosity to the thermal diffusivity of the fluid

Pr = kinematic viscosity /thermal diffusivity = ν/a = μ/ρ/k/ρCp = μCp/k

  • Cp is the fluid constant pressure specific heat;

  • k is the fluid thermal conductivity.

Péclet number

The Péclet number Pe is a dimensionless number defined as the ratio of the rate of advection (or convection) by the flow to the rate of diffusion (matter or heat):

Pe = Lu/D = flow velocity relative to diffusion velocity

where L is the characteristic length of the system, U is the flow velocity, and D is the diffusion coefficient.
