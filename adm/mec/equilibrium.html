Equilibrium

The general motion of a rigid body can be separated into two as translational motion of the center of mass and the rotational motion around the center of mass. We've already seen that the motion of the center of mass is determined by external forces MaCM = ΣFext where M is the mass of the body. We say that a body is in translational equilibrium when

ΣFext = 0

The second type of motion, rotational motion about the center of mass. We've seen that torques cause angular accelerations; so the condition for rotational equilibriumn is

Στext = 0

For a system to be in a state of static equilibrium, it must be under both translational and rotational equilibrium and the body is at rest relative to the observer and so has no translational or angular speed (i.e. vcm = 0 and ω =0)
