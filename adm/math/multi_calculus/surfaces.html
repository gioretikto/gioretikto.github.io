Surfaces in ℝ3

We have already studied parametric curves, which describe more general curves than those described by the graph of f(x). Similarly the graph of a function f(x,y) is a surface in ℝ3, but not every surface can be the graph of such functions e.g. the sphere is not the graph of a two-variable function. Indeed there is no general way to determine the graph of a surface assigned by a function f(x,y,z). To represent general surfaces we use a parametric representation as for curves, that is a function

r(t, u) : ℝ2 ⟶ ℝ3

with r = (x,y,z) and

{ x = x ( t , u ) y = y ( t , u ) ( t , u ) ∈ A z = z ( t , u )

with A a given region of the plane ℝ2.

Example 1. The sphere of radius R and centered in the origin has parametric representation

{ x = R sin ⁡ ϕ cos ⁡ θ y = R sin ⁡ ϕ sin ⁡ θ ϕ ∈ [ 0 , π ] , θ ∈ [ 0 , 2 π ) z = R cos ⁡ ϕ

with R a constant and the two parameters (φ, θ) respectively the colatitude and longitude.

It is necessary to specify the set in which the parameters vary. The same function represent with φ ∈ [0,π/4], θ ∈ [0,2π) a spherical cap while the conditions φ ∈ [0,π], θ ∈ [0,2π/6] just a slice on the sphere.
