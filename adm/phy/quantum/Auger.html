Auger Electron Spectroscopy

XPS and Auger electron spectroscopy (AES) are very closely related; in XPS well look at electrons directly ejected from the inner core of surface atoms. AES is based on a two-step process in which the first step involves the ioniziation of a core level by exposure of x-rays, or, more commonly, using an incident beam of electrons ( > 1keV), because high fluxes of high-energy electrons are easier to obtain. This vacant core level, A, is filled by electron of lower-binding energy, B, which may give up its energy to another electron in state C, which is ejected in the Auger effect, with kinetic energy

Ekin = EA − EB − EC

The energy released by the electron falling from state B into the core hole at A is given to an emitted photon with energy

hν = EA − EB

This is the same decay process as that occuiring in X-ray tube in which core holes in the target are created by energetic electron bomardment, leading to themission of characteristic lines (such as K&alkpha;).
