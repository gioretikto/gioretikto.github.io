Wheatstone Bridge

One method for determining the value of an unknown resistance R is by means of a simple Wheatstone bridge circuit (Fig.1). In the diagram shown above let us consider that R1 and R2 are the known resistors, R3 is variable resistor and R4 is the unknown say RX. variable resistance.

Under equilibrium conditions we must have zero current through CD, we must have VC − VD = 0 and as also the currents by Kirchoff's laws, through AC and CB must be the same, equal to i1 and the current through AD and DB must be equal to i2. Since

VC − VA = R1i1
VD − VA = R3i2

We thus have

VC − VD = VA − R1 i1 − (VA + R3 i2) = 0  ⟶  R3 i2 = R1 i1  3.3.1

But we have also

VB − VC = R2i1
VB − VD = Rxi2

Thus

VC = VB − R2i1
VD = VB − Rxi2

So VC − VD is also equal to

VC − VD = VB − R2i1 − (VB − Rxi2) = 0 ⟶  R2 i2 = Rx i1  3.3.2

If we divide Eq 3.3.1 by 3.3.2 we obtain

R3 i2/(R2 i2) = R1 i1 / (Rx i1)

So the equilibrium conditions can be expressed as

R1 / R2 = R3 / RX
