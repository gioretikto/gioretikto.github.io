Motion of a charged particle in a magnetic field

Motion in a uniform magnetic field, θ = π/2

Suppose that in a region of space there is a uniform and constant magnetic field B, and that the initial velocity of a charged particle is orthogonal to B: the Lorentz force is orthogonal to both B and v, so that v lies in the plane orhogonal to B. The motion of the particles occurs in this fixed plane and by setting sen θ = 1, we have

F = qvB = ma = m v2/r

which yield the radius of the path

r = m v q B = p q B

where p is the modulus of the momentum. The curvature, r is constant as B is constant and q and m are as well constant. The modulus of v does not change in modulus as the force is uniquly centripetal.
