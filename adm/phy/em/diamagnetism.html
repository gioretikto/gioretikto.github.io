Diamagnetism

To account for the lack of magnetism in the absence of an external magnetic field Hext, we assume the atom lacks a net magnetic dipole moment. This implies that before Hext is applied, the number of electrons orbiting in one direction is the same as that orbiting in the opposite direction, with the result that the net upward magnetic dipole moment of the atom equals the net downward magnetic dipole moment.

The origin of diamagnetism is the orbital motion of electrons in atoms under the influence of an applied magnetic field. The orbital motion of electron yields microscopic current loops that produce a magnetic field. These microscopic current loops tend to aling in such a manner that the magnetic field they produce opposes the applied magnetic field. Thus, the diamangetism resembles the induced electromotive force governed by Lenz's law.
