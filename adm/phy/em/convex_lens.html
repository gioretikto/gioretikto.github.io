Convex Surface, Low Refractive Index to High

Mathematically, examining image formation with thin lenses is slightly more difficult than with mirrors: Snell’s law isn’t quite as simple as the law of reflection. We'll approach the problem in two steps by starting with the effect of a single spherical surface before considering two spherical surfaces together.

It is far more convenient to begin the study of refractive optics with lenses in air - it explains the workings of common optical devices, and it is vastly easier to perform experiments with light sources, lenses, and screens on a table than to devise experiments with a single curved, refracting boundary. However, single surfaces still create the illusion that an object being observed is now different in size or placement: looking into a curved fishbowl is one example.
