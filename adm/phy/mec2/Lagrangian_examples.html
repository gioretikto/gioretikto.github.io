Applications of Lagrangian Mechanics

Pendulum

Consider a pendulum with bob mass m suspended from a fixed point by the massless string of lengt l, the whole acted upon by a constant froce of gravity g and let the generalized coordinate the angle 𝜃 between the string and the vertical.

y = −l cos θ
x = l sin θ
V = mgy = −mgl cos θ
V(θ) −mgl cos θ
T = m/2(ẋ2 + ẏ2)
ẋ = d/dt (l sin θ) = l cos θ θ̇
ẏ = d/dt (l cos θ) = −l sin θ θ̇
T(θ̇) = ½ ml2θ̇2

∂ L ∂ θ − d d t ∂ L ∂ θ ˙ = 0

L = ml2θ̇2/2 + mgl cos θ
∂L/∂θ = −mgl sin θ
d/dt (∂L/∂θ̇) = ml2θ̈

The equation of motion is

−mgl sin θ − ml2θ̈ = 0
θ̈ = −g/l ⋅ sin θ
