Hausdorff Spaces

Definition A space X is said to be Hausdorff space if for any two distinct points x,y ∈ X there are open neighboorhods Ux and Uy of x and y, respectively, such that Ux ∩ Uy = ∅.

For example, every metric space (X,d) is a Hausdorff space because for every two distinct points x,y ∈ X open balls Ux = Br(x) and Uy = Br(y) do not intersect if r < d(x,y)/2.
