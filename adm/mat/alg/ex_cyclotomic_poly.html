Exercise on cyclotomic polynomials

  1. Prove a) that the product of two nth roots of unity is again an nth root of unity, b) and the inverse of a nth root of unity is is a nth root of unity.

  2. Let 𝐶n the set of the nth roots of unity. Prove the existence of a bijection f between 𝐶n and ℤn, such that f(ζ1ζ2) = f(ζ1) + f(ζ2) for every ζ1,ζ2 ∈ 𝐶n.

  3. Prove that for every d|n there exists a d-th root of the unit that has d has order. Count for each d how many n-th roots of the unit having the same order d are there.

  4. Evaluate the unique factorization in terms of irreducible factors over ℚ of the following polynomials:

    x18 − 1,   x20 − 1,   x21 − 1,   x30 − 1.

Solutions

  1. If αn = 1 and βn = 1 , then (αβ)n = αnβn = 1. If ζn = 1 taking the −1 power of both sides we get (1/ζ)n = 1.

  2. Note that e^x e^y = e^(x + y) for all x, y. Define f: 𝐶n ⟶ ℤn as f(ζk) = [k]; e.g. if we take 𝐶2 = {−1,1} and ℤ2={[0],[1]}, then f(−10)= [0] and f(11)=[1]

  3. It sufficies to consider ζn/d, with ζ primitive root of xn − 1 because

    (ζ(n/d))d = ζn = 1.

    ζ(n/d) has order d. For every d|n there are φ(d) primitive n-th roots of unity with order d.

  4. x18 − 1 = Φ1 ⋅ Φ2 ⋅ Φ3 ⋅ Φ6 ⋅ Φ9 ⋅ Φ18 = (x − 1) (x + 1) (x2 + x + 1) (x2 −x +1) (x6 + x3 + 1) (x6 − x3 + 1). Where Φ18 can be obtained by long division oi x18 − 1 by the other lnown cyclotomic polynomials.

    x20 − 1 = Φ1 ⋅ Φ2 ⋅ Φ4 ⋅ Φ5 ⋅ Φ10 ⋅ Φ20 = (x − 1) (x + 1) (x2 + 1) (x4 + x3 + x2 + x + 1)(x4 − x3 + x2 −x +1)(x8 −x6 + x4 − x2 +1).

    x21 − 1 = Φ1 ⋅ Φ3 ⋅ Φ7 ⋅ Φ21 = (x − 1) (x2 + x + 1) (x6 + x5 + x4 +x3 + x2 + x +1) (x12 − x11 + x9 − x8 + x6 − x4 + x3 −x +1). ■
