Tensors

The more formal definition of tensor is that of collection of vector and covectors combined together using the tensor product.

The basic idea is that a tensor is a scalar-valued multilinear forms with variables in both V and V*, that is multilinear forms acting on Cartesian products of a vector space and its dual. A (m n) tensors is a function, linear in each argument, which takes p one-forms and q vectors as arguments and maps them to a scalar.

Definition 8.2.1. A function

T : V ∗ × ⋯ × V ∗ ⏟ p  copies × V × ⋯ × V ⏟ q  copies → F

is said a tensor T of type (or rank) (p, q). The space of all tensors of a fixed type (p, q) forms a vector space; they can be added together and multiplied by real numbers.

For example we can write the (2 1) tensor T as

T( ⋅̃, ⋅̃, ⋅̅)

Recalling that the elements of V* are maps V ⟶ 𝔽, hence a dual vector is a tensor of type (0,1).
A vector is an element of V, hence (up to a canonical isomorphism) an element of V**, hence an element of V* ⟶ 𝔽, hence a tensor of type (1,0). A scalar is a type (0, 0) tensor.

Similarly n x x matrices A, are examples of (1 1) tensors, if you left-multiply a matrix by a row vector, and right-multiply it by a column vector, then you end up with a scalar:

wT A v

this means that the n-component row vectors are the one-forms in the vector space dual to the column vectors. For example, we can think of A as a map, A(w, v) = Aij wi vj, assigning a number to a pair comprising a dual vector and vector. In index-free notation, this reads as wTAv, where w is a row vector with covariant components wi.

Tensor Product

The tensor product is a way to make a new vector space out of two given ones. This operation can be thought of as a generalization of the Kronecker product that not only lets us multiply together vectors (from 𝔽n) and matrices of different sizes, but also allows us to multiply together vectors from any vector spaces.

Tthe tensor product V ⊗ W of two vector spaces V and W (over the same field) is a vector space to which is associated a bilinear map V × W → V ⊗ W that maps a pair (v, w), v ∈ V, w ∈ W to an element of V ⊗ W denoted v ⊗ w, so that the following properties hold for all v,x ∈ V, w,y ∈ W, and c ∈ 𝔽

  • v ⊗ (w + y) = (v ⊗ w) + (v ⊗ y)

  • (v + x) ⊗ w = (v ⊗ w) + (x ⊗ w)

  • (cv) ⊗ w = v ⊗ (cw) = c(v ⊗ w).

In other words, we would like the tensor product to be bilinear.

For each v = ∑i ci ei ∈ V and w = ∑j dj fj ∈ W (where ci, dj ∈ 𝔽, ei ∈ B, and fj ∈ B' for all i and j) we then similarly define

v ⊗ w = ∑ni=1 ∑mj=1 (ei ⊗ fj)
