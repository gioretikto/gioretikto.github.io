Change of Basis

When we change the basis B that we are working with, the resulting coordinate vectors change as well. We want to study the relation between components of vectors represented with respect two different bases.
Let V a vector space of dimension n over ℝ and let B = {v1, ..., vn} and B̄ = {v̄1, ..., v̄n} two bases of V. Then the vectors v̄1, ..., v̄n can be written as linear combination of the vectors of B as follows

v ¯ 1 = a 11 v 1 + a 12 v 2 + … + a 1 n v n v ¯ 2 = a 21 v 1 + a 22 v 2 + … + a 2 n v n ⋮ v ¯ n = a n 1 v 1 + a n 2 v 2 + … + a n n v n

We observe that if

A = ( a 11 a 12 … a 1 n a 21 a 22 … a 2 n ⋮ ⋮ ⋮ a n 1 a n 2 … a n n )

by Theorem 5.1.1, it results det A ≠ 0, because the vectors v̄1, ..., v̄n are linear independent, hence A is invertible. The following relation hold

( v ¯ 1 v ¯ 2 ⋮ v ¯ n ) = A ( v 1 v 2 ⋮ v n ) ( v 1 v 2 ⋮ v n ) = A − 1 ( v ¯ 1 v ¯ 2 ⋮ v ¯ n )
