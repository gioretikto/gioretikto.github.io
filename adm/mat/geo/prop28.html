Proposizione I.28

Proposizione I.28 Consider two lines with a transversal. If either of the following conditions holds, then the two lines are parallel.

  1. An exterior angle and an opposite interior angle on the same side of the transversal are congruent.

  2. Two interior angles on the same side of the transversal are supplementary.

Proof - We assume (1) first. Referring to Fig., suppose the exterior angle ∠EGB is congruent to the opposite interior angle ∠GHD. By the Vertical Angle Theorem, ∠EGB ≌ ∠AGH. This gives us alternate angles ∠AGH ≌ ∠GHD so by Prop. I.27 AB and CD are parallel.

Next assume (2). Say that ∠BGH + ∠GHD = π. By Prop. I.13, we also have ∠AGH + ∠BGH = π. This gets us ∠AGH ≌ ∠GHD by CN3. Since ∠AGH and ∠GHD are alternate, the result follows by Prop. I.27. □
