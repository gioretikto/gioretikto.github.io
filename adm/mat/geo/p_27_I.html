Proposizione I.27

The first two propositions in the next thread describe sufficient conditions for lines to be parallel. Neither uses Postulate V.

Proposizione I.27 If two lines are intersected by a transversal that forms congruent alternate angles, then the lines are parallel.

Proof - Let lines AB, CD be given and let EF be a transversal. Assume alternate angles ∠AEF and ∠EFD are congruent. If AB and CD are not parallel, they must meet in the direction of A and C, or the direction of B and D. Suppose they meet in the direction of B and D at a point G. Since ∠AEF is exterior to the triangle ΔGEF, by Prop I.16, it must exceed ∠EFG = ∠EFD, a contradiction. The argument that the lines cannot meet in the direction of A and C is similar. We conclude that AB and CD are parallel. □
