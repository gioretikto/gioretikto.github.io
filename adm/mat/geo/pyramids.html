Pyramids

Pyramids are polyhedrons. The base of a pyramid is a polygon, and the three or more triangular faces of the pyramid meet at a point known as the apex. The most common type of pyramid that is usually encountered, are pyramids with a square base. There are also triangular based pyramids, tetrahedrons, and hexagonal-based pyramids and so on.

Derive Volume of Square based Pyramid

If we first consider a standard cube, where all sides are the same length. The volume of this cube is given by a × a × a, or a3.

As the volume of the cube is given by a3, and as the pyramid is one of 6 equal sized pyramids that can fit inside the cube. The volume of this individual pyramid can be given by a3/6.

The pyramid has a height h, which is half the length of a cube side a. As the top of the pyramid is the center of the cube So with h = a/2​, it's the case that 2h = a. Putting this Information Together

V = a3/6 = (a × a2)/6 = (2h × a2)/6 = 2ha2/6 = ha2/3
