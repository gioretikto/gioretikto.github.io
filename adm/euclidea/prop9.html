Proposizione I.9

Proposition I.9. An angle can be bisected..

Proof Let ∠ACB be given. Choose D on CA and E on CB so that CD ≌ CE. Construct ΔDEF, an equilateral triangle on DE. We claim that CF bisects ∠ACB. By the construction, CD ≌ CE, CF = CF, and FD ≌ FE so by SSS, ΔCDF ≌ ΔCEF. As corresponding angles, ∠FCD ≌ ∠FCE. □

pBoth the segment CF and the ray CF constructed in the proof of Prop. I.9 are called the angle bisector for ∠ACB. We leave it as an exercise to show that the angle bisector is essentially unique.
