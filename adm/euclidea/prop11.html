Proposizione I.11

Proposition I.11. We can construct a perpendicular to any given line at any point on that line.

Proof Suppose we want a perpendicular at C, a point on a line segment AB. Take D different from C but otherwise arbitrary on AC. Choose E on CB so that CE ≌ CD. Construct an equilateral triangle, ΔDEF, on DE. By SAS, ΔDFC ≌ ΔEFC so ∠DCF ≌ ∠ECF. As congruent adjacent angles, ∠DCF and ∠ECF must be right, so CF is perpendicular to AB.□
