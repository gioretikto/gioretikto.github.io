Similar Triangles

Proposition (AAA similary theorem VI.4) Equiangular triangles are similar.

This definition is known as the Angle-Angle-Angle (AAA) criterion for triangle similarity.

Proposition 20.4 (Sim SAS) (VI.6) Suppose that two triangles ABC and A'B'C' have the angles at A and A' equal, and the two sides AB, AC are proportional to the two sides A'B', A'C'. Then the two triangles are similar.

When we enlarge or reduct a shape by a scale factor, the original and the image have corresponing angles equal and corresponding sides in same ratio or proportion.

Theorem 1 (Proposition 5, Book VI of The Elements) If two triangles are similar, i.e. they are equiangular, then the lengths of their corresponding sides are proportional.

AB/A'B' = BC/B'C' = AC/A'C'

It follows immediately from Theorem 1 that if ΔABC ~ ΔA'B'C then there is a positive number λ, called the scale factor, such that

AB = λ ⋅ A'B',   BC = λ ⋅ B'C'   and AC = λ ⋅ A'C'.

The converse to this proposition is also true. That is, we have the

Theorem 2 (Proposition 4, Book VI of The Elements.) If two triangles have corresponding sides which are proportional. Then their corresponding angles are equal. That is, the two triangles are similar

While congruent triangles have the same size and shape, similar triangles have the same shape but not necessarily the same size.

Theorem. Every parallel to one side of a triangle forms a triangle with the other two sides which is similar to the first.

To recap, when two triangles are similar we find that:

  • their corresponding angles are equal (they are equi-angular);

  • their corresponing sides are in the same ratio.
