Proposizione I.20

The Triangle Inequality is one of the most important devices for studying the real line. For Euclid, it is strictly about the sides of a triangle. Here we use formal sums of segments for the first time.

Proposizione I.20 - (Triangle Inequality). The length of one side of a triangle is less than the sum of the two remaining sides.

Proof - Consider a triangle ΔABC. Extend BA through the point D, D not on AB but chosen so that AD ≌ AC, as in Figure. Join points D and C. By Pons Asinorum, ∠ADC ≌ ∠ACD. By CN5, ∠BCD > ∠ACD, thus ∠BCD > ∠ADC. Considering Prop. I.19 applied to ΔBCD, we have BD > BC. Then BD = BA + AD ≌ BA + AC > BC. Similarly we can prove that the sum of AB and BC is also greater than CA, and the sum of BC and CA is greater than AB.□
