Area of an Oblique Triangle and the Sine Law

An oblique triangle is a triangle with no right angles.

Noting that each triangle in Fig.1a has height h, the area of each triangle is

Area = 1/2 (base) (height) = 1/2 ⋅ a ⋅ c sin B = ac sin B/2

By similar argument, you can develop the formulas

Area = 1/2 ⋅ bc sin A = ab sin C/2

To calculate the area of the obtuse triangle in Fig. 1b, use the reference angle 180° − C and the difference formula for sine, as follows

h = b sin(180° − C) = b (sin 180° cos C − cos 180° sin C) = b [0 ⋅ cos C − (−1) ⋅ sin C] = b sin C

so the area is

Area = ab sin C/2

We conclude that

The area of any triangle is one-half the product of the lenghts of two sides times the sine of their included angle. That is,

Area = bc sin A/2 = ab sin C/2 = ac sin B/2   1.4.1

Example 1. Find the area of a triangle with thow sides of lenght 80 meters and 46.22 meters and an included angle of 102 °

Solution. The are is

Area = (ab sin C)/2 = 1/2 ⋅ (80 ⋅ 46.22) sin (102) = 1808.40 m2  ■

The law of Sines

The law of sines states that in any triangle the ratio of the lenght of a side to the sine of the opposite angle is the same for all three sides, so in a given triangle this ratio is constant. Considering Fig.1, the Law of Sines states that in triable ABC

sin A/a = sin B/b = sin C/c

Proof. It can be easily obtained by 1.4.1 dividing it, say by bc.  □
