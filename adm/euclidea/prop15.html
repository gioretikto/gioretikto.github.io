Proposizione I.15

Vertical angles are non-adjacent angles formed by two intersecting lines. The terminology is not defined explicitly but is used in Heath’s translation of The Elements. In Fig. 1, ∠AEC and ∠BED are vertical angles, as ∠AED and ∠BEC.

Proposition I.15. (Vertical Angle Theorem). Vertical angles formed by the intersection of two lines are congruent.

Proof. Let lines AB, CD intersect at E. By Prop. I.13, ∠AEC and ∠AED are supplementary. Similarly, ∠AEC and ∠BEC are supplementary. It follows by CN3 that ∠AED ≌ ∠BEC. The same argument applies to show that ∠AEC ≌ ∠BED. □
