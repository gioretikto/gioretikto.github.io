Congiuzioni y, e

There are two words in Spanish for the words and: y and e. We use e when the word that follows begins with the sound [i], which corresponds to the letters i and hi. (Remember that h is always silent.).

  • Daniel e Isabel

  • Padre e hijo

There are a few words that begin with hie; in this case hies sounds like [ye] instead of [ie], and the conjunction y is used.

  • Agua y hielo

There are two words in Spanish for the word or: o and u. We use u when the word that follows begins with the soun [o], which corresponds to the letters o and ho. If the words begins with the diphtong hue as in hueso, «bone» we use o.

  • siete u ocho

  • minutos u horas

  • músculo o huesos

There are three ways to translate the words but: pero, sino and sino que.

In order to use sino, two conditions are required:

  1. The first part of the sentence must be negative:

    • Ese muchacho no es Pedro sino Pablo. (That boy is not Pedro but Pablo).

  2. The two parts of the sentence must be parallel - that is, the constrast must be between two nouns, two adjectives, and so on. At the same time those two parts must be on the same level of meaning: two colors, two religions, two affiliations. Otherwise pero must be used.

    • No es gorda sino delgada (She's not fat but thin): Fatness and thinnes are on the same level.

    • No es gordo pero es altro (He's not fat but tall): Fatness and height are on different levels

Sino que is used when each of the two parts of the sentence contains a subject and a verb.

  • Pepe no fuma sino que bebe (Joe doesn't smoke, but he drinks)

  • Pepe no quiere fumar sino bebe (Joe doesn't smoke, but he drinks)
