A little, a few

When we want to indicate "a small quantity of" or "a small number of", we use a little (with uncountable) and a few (with countable nouns).

  • There's a little cheese in the kitchen

  • There are a few pupils in the classroom

  • There are a few children on the beach

Lttle and few, without a, are more negative ideas. Littles mean "almost no"; few means "not many":

  • There's little cheese in the kitchen

  • There are few children on the beach
