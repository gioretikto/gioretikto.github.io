Preposition by and on

BY: the method or instrument of transportation

The preposition "By" can be used to distinguish between methods of trasportation, bicycle, car, train, etc.:

  • My friends drove home, but I went on foot. Mary went by bus.

  • They are going by bicycle.

  • I go to school by bike every day.

We generally ride "ON" or "In" a vehicle, as in:

  • I ride on a bus

  • I ride on a bike

It would be awkward, for example to say: "I ride by a bus".

We do not use by when the reference if to a specific bicycle, car, train, etc.

  • Mary went there on my bike (not: by my bike).

  • We travelled in Mr. Joshis's car (not: by Mr. Joshi's car)

The preposizion ON

By designates something that will take you to your destination. For instance, I don't see how you can travel by foot - unless someone else's foot is transporting you! Therefore, you say:

  • He goes to the office on foot

  • She used to go to school on roller skates.

  • I traveled on skis from France to Switzerland.
