The Irregular Verb volo

The irregular verb volo, vis, volui, velle, «I will », and its compounds nolo, non vis, nolui, nolle, «I will not», and malo, mavis, malui, malle, «I will rather», «I prefer» in addition to be athematic in the present stem, mostra anche altre anomalie: ha alter- nanza vocalica nel tema del presente, manca del supino e dei tempi da esso derivati, ha le desinenze del congiuntivo presente in -im, -is, -it ecc. (derivate da un antico ottativo). I tempi derivati dal tema del perfetto sono invece regolari, pertanto presentiamo qui solo la coniugazione dei tempi derivati dal tema del presente.

Notes

The stem of volo presents qualitative apophony vel-/vol-, in particular:

  • The stem vel- is used before a or l.

  • The stem vol- is used before vowels different from i. In ancient latin this stem was also used before consonants (volt, voltis), but in classical latin the o through the apophony phenomenon became u (vul, vultis)

  • The 2nd person of the indicative vis derives form another root, which can be found in the adjective in-vi-tus, «which doesn't want» and replaced the original form of the 2nd person vel, which became a conjunction "or".

The athematic forms are:

  • The 2nd and 3rd persons singular (vi-s, vul-t) and the 2nd plural (vul-tis) of the present indicative.

  • The present subjunctive vel-im, vel-is, etc and imperfect vel-e-m, etc
