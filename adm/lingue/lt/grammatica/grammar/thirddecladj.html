Third Declension Adjectives

While some adjectives use the endings of the first and second declensions, likewise, we shall see that a large group of adjectives belongs to the third declension and use the endings of the third declension only. For this they are called third declension adjectives; Theese adjectives are divided in three gorups depending on the number of endings they present in the nominative singular:

  • Adjectives having three terminations in the the nominative singular, one for each gender; like:

    acer (m.), acris (f.), acre (n.), «sharp», «sour, bitter»;

    celeber (m.), celebris (f.), celebre (n.), «celebrated, famous»;

  • Adjectives having two terminations in the the nominative singualr; one for the masculine and feminine and one for the neuter gender; like:

    facilis (m. and f.), facile (n.), «easy»;

    brevis (m. and f.), breve (n.), «short».

    crudelis (m. and f.), crudele (n.), «cruel, bloody».

    fidelis (m. and f.), fidele (n.), «faithful».

  • Adjectives having one terminations in the nominative singular, for all three genders; vocabulary entries for this adjectives give the nominative singular followed by the genitive singular, like:

    audax, audacis, (m., f. and n.) «bold, daring»;

    dilĭgens, dilĭgēntis', «diligent, careful»;
