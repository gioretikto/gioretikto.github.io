Conjunctions

A conjunction (from coniungo, «to join, connect») is a invariable part of the speech employed to connect different words or sentences.

When the connection is between the principal and dependent sentences, the conjunction is called subordinative conjunction. Otherwise they are known as coordinate sentences (coordinate means of the same rank in the sentence).

Coordinative Conjunctions

Depending on their function, coodinative conjunctions are distinguished in:

Copulatives

They connect words thar are to to be considere joinlty

  • et, atque, ac, (only before consonant), -que (enclitic), and

  • etiam, quoque, (Quoque follows the word which it connects), also:

    • Tu quoque contra me es! - Also you, againts me!

    • Is quoque - He also

And the negatives:

  • neque, nec, neither, not

  • ne... quidem, not even

Disjunctives

They connect things that are to be considered separately.

  • aut (opposition between two terms mutually exclusive), either, or

  • vel (choice between two terms)

  • sive or seu before consonant, or, or rather

    • Deus sive natura - "God or nature", "God, that is to say, Nature"

Adversatives

They express opposition

  • sed, vērum, cetĕrum, but, on the contrary

  • at, atqui but, yet

  • vero, autem (postponed to one or more words) but, on the contrary, truly

  • tamen, attămen, nevertheless, however, yet

  • vēruntamen, yet, although.

Causal

They express cause or reason

  • nam, namque, etĕnim (at the beginning of a clause), for

  • enim (after one or more words), for

Inferential

  • ităque, ergo, igĭtur (postponed), therefore, then

  • proĭnde, on that account

  • idĕo, idcirco, propterĕa therefore, for that cause

  • quāre(or qua re), quamŏbrem, quocirca, wherefore, for which reason

Limitative

  • quidem (postponed), indeed

  • equĭdem (usually with the verb in the 1st sing. pers.)

Corrective

  • quin, quin etiam, no, rather

  • immo, immo vero, or ratheractually

  • quamquam, although

Correlatives

  • et... et, both ... and

  • nec... nec, neither ... nor

  • neque... neque, neither ... nor

  • cum... tum, while ... at the same time ,as ... so

  • nunc... nunc, now ... now

  • tum... tum, not only ... but also

  • modo... modo, now ... now

  • non solum... sed, not only ... but

  • non solum... sed etiam, not only ... but also

  • non modo... sed, not only ... but also

  • non modo... sed etiam, not only ... but also

  • non tantum... sed etiam, not only ... but also
