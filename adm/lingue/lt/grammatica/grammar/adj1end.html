Adjectives of one ending

Third declension Adjectives with one ending have one ending in the nominative singular for the masculine and feminine genders, and one ending for the neuter gender; They are declined like dilĭgens, dilĭgēntis, «diligent, careful»:

  Singular Plural
Case Masculine/Fem Neuter Masculine/Fem Neuter
Nominative dilĭgens dilĭgens dilĭgēntēs dilĭgēntiă
Genitive dilĭgēntĭs dilĭgēntĭs dilĭgēntĭŭm dilĭgēntĭŭm
Dative dilĭgēntī dilĭgēntī dilĭgēntĭbŭs dilĭgēntĭbŭs
Accusative dilĭgēntĕm dilĭgens dilĭgēntēs dilĭgēntiă
Vocative dilĭgens dilĭgens dilĭgēntēs dilĭgēntiă
Ablative dilĭgēntī dilĭgēntī dilĭgēntĭbŭs dilĭgēntĭbŭs

As can be noticed the declension is equal for the masculine adn feminine, whilist the neuter follows the declension for neuter nouns. Other examples of adjectives with one termination are:

  • ingens, ingentis, «big, huge»

  • velox, velōcis, «fast, swift»

  • ferox, ferōcis, «haughty»

  • felix, felīcis, «happy»

  • sagax, sagācis, «sagacious, acute»

It is decline as an adjective of the second class of one ending also the present participle.
