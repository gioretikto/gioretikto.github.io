Nominative + Infinitive construction of verba narrandi, iubendi and putandi

The nominative with the infinitive is also used with the passive of the verbs of saying, thinking, believing. As videor the following verbs are used in the personal construction:

  • verba narrandi, such as narror, feror, trador, dicor, nuntior “it is narrated/related, I am said to ...”;

  • verba iubendi and prohibendi, as iubeor “I am commanded”, vetor, prohibeor “I am forbidden”;

  • verba putandi, as existimor, putor, habeor, credor, iudicor “it is believed that I”:

    • Brutum et Cassium laudavisse dicor (Cic.) - I am said to have praised Brutus and Cassius

    • Hannibal redire in Africam iubetur. (Eutr.) - Hannibal receives the order to return to Africa.

    • Nos a consulibus Capuam venire iussi sumus (Cic.) - We were ordered by the consuls to go to Capua

    • Lycurgi temporibus Homerus fuisse traditur (Cic) - Homer is related to have existed in the times of Lycurgus

verba declarandi and sentiendi: Impersonal construction

In the passive perfect and tenses derived from it and in the passive Periphrastic, the verba narrandi and putandi, prefer the impersonal construction (dictum est, tradĭtum est, putandum est, etc.), with the accusative and infinitive:

  • Traditum est etiam Homerum caecum fuisse (Cic.) - It has been related that Homer was blind, too.

  • Cleopatra perisse morsu aspidis putabatur (Suet.) - It was thought that Cleopatra had died from the bite of an aspid

  • Dictum est igitur ab eruditissimis viris nisi sapientem liberum esse neminem (Cic.) - Therefore it has been said by the most educated men that no one can be free unless he is wise

  • Ubi tyrannus est, ibi non vitiosam, sed dicendum est plane nullam esse rem publicam (Cic. Rep. iii. 43) - Wwhere there is a tyrant, it must be said, not that the commonwealth is evil, but that it does not exist at all.

It is also possible to find the active third person plural of this verbs (dicunt, ferunt, tradunt, etc) :

  • Latinum pacem cum Aenea iunxisse tradunt (Livy) - Some report that Latinus, made peace with Aeneas
