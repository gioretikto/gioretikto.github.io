Accent

Accent is the vocal emphasis placed on a particular syllable of a word. There are 3 fundmental rules governing accents:

  1. The last syllable will never carry the accent unless it is a one-syllable word, as in: réx, hòc, tè, nè, sì. In words containing two syllables, the accent falls always on the penultimate:

    • felix, /félix/

    • păter, /pàter/

    • lābor, /làbor/

  2. The accent can only fall on one of the last three syllables of a word.

  3. Latin words are accented according to the duration of the penultimate syllable: the accent falls on the penultimate syllable if this is long and on the antepenultimate if the penultimate is short:

    Penultimate long

    • vi‐vĕ‐re, /vìvere/;

    • a-mā‐re, /amàre/;

    • tribūnal, /tribùnal/

    Penultimate short

    • genĭtor, génitor

To display a certain command of the latin language, it is important to pronounce the word by stressing the accented syllable as dictated by the rule above and not according the caprice of the speaker. It is common and unpleasant to hear the famous sentence Dīvĭdĕ et ĭmpĕrā (Dìvide et ìmpera) mispronounced.

Enclitic Accent

When an enclitic like -que («and»), -ve («or»), -ne (interrogative particle), is joined to a word, the accent falls on the syllable next before the enclitic:

  • Senatus populusque (pron. Senátus Populúsque); (The senate and the people)

  • Māter filiăque (pro. màter filiàque), «The mother and the daughter»;

  • Rosa violăve (pron. ròsa violàve), «The rose or the violet»

The word ităque is pronounced “itàque” when has the meaning of "thus", because composed of ita + que; It has to be pronounced as “ìtaque” when has the meaning "therefore", "then".
