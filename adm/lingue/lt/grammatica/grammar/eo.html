Notes

As eo is an intranstive verb, it has a passive only in the 3rd person singular- i.e. an impersonal passive, ītur, ībatur, ībitur, itum est, itum erat, etc, eatus, īretur, etc, eundum est, īri.

The compounds of eo

In like manner are conjugated all the compounds of eo. While eo is intranstive some of its compounds are transitive thanks to the preposition and consequenlty have the passive voice in all its forms.

ab-ĕo, -is, abĭi, abĭtum, abīre, «I go away»

ad-ĕo, -is, adĭi, adĭtum, adīre, «I go to»; with transitive meaning, «to undertake», «to approach» have a regular complete passive voice.

antĕ-ĕo, -is, anteĭi, anteĭtum, anteīre, «I go before»; with transitive meaning, «superare», (pron. antèeo, ànteis, antèii, antèitum, anteìre)

circŭm-ĕo, -is, circumĭi, circumĭtum, circumīre, «I go round»; with transitive meaning, «circondare» (pron. circùmeo, cìrcumis, circùmii, circùmitum, circumìre)

co-ĕo, -is, coĭi, coĭtum, coīre, «I unite with»; with transitive meaning, «I join, ally with».

ex-ĕo, -is, exĭi, exĭtum, exīre, «I go out»

in-ĕo, -is, inĭi, inĭtum, inīre, «I enter, go into»; with transitive meaning, «I take up (an office, position)».

intĕr-ĕo, -is, interĭi, interĭtum, interīre, «I am ruined, undone», «I die»

ob-ĕo, -is, obĭi, obĭtum, obīre, «I go towards»; with transitive meaning, «I encounter»

per-ĕo, -is, perĭi, (part. fut. periturus), perīre, «I perish, pass away, die.»; used as passive of perdo («I destroy, ruin.»), of which only the passive forms perdĭtus and perdendus

prae-ĕo, -is, praeĭi, praeĭtum, praeīre, «I precede» are used.

praetĕr-ĕo, -is, praeterĭi, praeterĭtum, praeterīre, «I pass by or go by»; with transitive meaning, «I disregard», «I neglect, omit or miss»

prod-ĕo, -is, prodĭi, prodĭtum, prodīre, «I come forth, appear»

red-ĕo, -is, redĭi, redĭtum, redīre, «I return»

sub-ĕo, -is, subĭi, subĭtum, subīre, «I go under»; with transitive meaning, «to undergo»

trans-ĕo, -is, transĭi, transĭtum, transīre, «I pass over»; with transitive meaning, «I traverse»

vēn-ĕo, -is, venĭi, vēnīre, «I am sold (as a slave)» is a compound of venum + eo («to go on sale»), it is used as passive of vendo (compund of venum + do, «I sell, vend»), which has only the passive forms vendĭtus and vendendus; it is important to note that vēnire («I am sold», from vēneo) is different from vĕnire («to come», from vĕnio) by the quantity of the root vowel; Thus it is:

  • vēnis, vēnit, «you are sold», «he is sold»;

  • vĕnis, vĕnit, «you come», «he comes».

There is also a compound of eo, ambio, -is, ambĭi (o ambīvi), ambītum, ambīre (from amb + eo), «I round, go round, pass around,», which follows regularly the 4th conjugation: imperfect amb-iebam, amb-iebas, amb-iebat, future amb-iam, amb-ies, amb-iet etc.

Two compunds of eo, per-eo «I perish», vaene-eo, «I go for sale», are used as passives of corresponding compounds of do, per-do «I ruin», ven-do, «I sell», though perditus, venditus, are also used.
