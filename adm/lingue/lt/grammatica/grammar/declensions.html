Latin Declensions

As stated before Latin is an inflected language. Inflection is a change made in the form of a word to show the grammatical function performed by that word in a phrase, clause, or sentence. Thhe inflection of a noun, adjective or pronoun is called declension and the inflection of a verb is called conjugation. A declension is a group of nouns that change their forms in the same way when their function in the sentence changes.

Root, stem, base

The generic meaning of a word is contained in its root. A root may be defined as the simplest element common to all words of a related group. For example the words of the group:

amicus, a male friend

amica, a female fiend.

amicitia, firendship

amatus, beloved

amor, love

amare, to love

inamabĭlis, unlovely

They all have the same root "am", and they all refer to the general idea either of love or fiendship. The meaning of a word is further modified into a stem, by certain prefixes or suffixes.

By adding the suffix "ic" to the root "am", we get the stem "amic-", containing the idea of friendship, to this and ending is added composed by the suffix "iti" and by the desinence "a". If the root "am" is added to the stem-vowel "o", or "a" , we get the stems "amo" and "ama", wich contain the idea of love.
