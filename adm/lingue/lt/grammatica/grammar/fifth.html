The fifth Declension

The nouns of the fifth declension are known by the genitive singular ending in -ei. In the nominative singualr they end in es, and the thematic vowel -ê- is transparent in all the case endings. The nouns of the fifth declensions never have stems that are not the same as the nominative singular.

Only a few nouns belong to the fifth declension, and they are declined as dies, diēi, m., «day» and res, rei, f., «thing»:

Case Singular Plural
Nominative diēs diēs
Genitive diēi diērŭm
Dative diēi diēbŭs
Accusative diĕm diēs
Vocative diēs diēs
Ablative diē diēbŭs

Case Singular Plural
Nominative rēs rēs
Genitive rĕi rērŭm
Dative rĕī rēbŭs
Accusative rĕm rēs
Vocative rēs rēs
Ablative rē rēbŭs

The only complete nouns of this declension are dies, and res, the other nouns are declined only in the singular except for the following nouns which have also the nominative, accusative and vocative plural:

acies, aciēi, sf., "battle line"

effigies, effigiēi, f., «effigy»;

facies, faciēi, f., «face»;

species,speciēi, f., «a seeing, view, look»

spes, spei, f., «hope»

All the nouns of this declension are feminine, except dies, and its compoun meridies, «mid-day, the south». Meridies is always masculine, dies is masculine in the plural, and in the singular is masculine except for the following cases:

  • whent it indicated a fixed day, an appointed time

    dies dicta, dies constituta, dies certa, «The fixed day».

    damnosa dies, «a time of suffering»;

    Die dicta legati reverterunt - «The fixed day the ambassadors came back»;

  • When it indicates the date of a letter:

    dies adscripta - «The date signed in the letter»

    In epistula dies signata non erat. «The date was not signed in the letter»;

  • when it signifies a time or duration:

    longa dies, «a long period»;

    dies perexigua, «a very brief period»

    Longa dies incertat animum- «A long period makes the soul suffering».

Some nouns of the fifth declension ending in -ies in the nominative singular, have also the form ending in -ia, of the first declension:

pigrities / pigritia, «laziness»;

mollities / mollitia, «la mollezza»;

materies / materia, «la materia»;

barbaries / barbaria, «la barbarie»;

avarities / avaritia, «greed, avarice»;

benevolenties / benevolentia, «kindness»;

blandities / blanditia, «flattery, compliment»;

these nouns in the genitive and dative singular have only the forms of the first declension in -ae: pigritiae, mollitiae, barbariae. etc.

The noun res, express the general meaning of "thing"; its meaning can be rendered more specific when followed by an adjective:

res divina - "The sacrifice"

res familiaris - "The heritage, family assets"

res frumentaria - "The supply (of wheat"

res militaris- "strategy, military art"

res publica - "The State"

res Romana - "The Roman State"

res rustica - "The Agricolture"

For the sake of clarity, let's see how res publica is declined:

Case Singular Plural
Nominative rēs publică rēs publicae
Genitive rĕī publicae rērŭm publicārŭm
Dative rĕī publicae rēbŭs publicīs
Accusative rĕm publicăm rēs publicās
Vocative rēs publică rēs publicae
Ablative rē publicā rēbŭs publicīs

Whes res is declined in the plural and followed up by adjectives or participles, some nouns can present only the plurarl (pluralia tantum), for example:

res adversae -"failures", "unfavorable situations";

res futurae - "the future";

res gestae - "le gesta", "the companies";

res nauticae - "the navy", "the naval art";

res novae- "the revolution", "political subversion";

res prosperae - "Teh prosperity";

res secundae - "The successes", "favorable situations".

Also the noun acies, has different meaning:

acie dimicare - "to fight in the open field";

acie excedere - "to leave the battlefield";

aciem instruere - "to deploy the army ready for the battle";

novissima acies - "the rearguard";

prima acies- "The vanguard."
