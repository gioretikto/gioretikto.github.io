Latin First Declension

The nouns belonging to the first declension have stem ends in "a", which is obtained by dropping the ending from the full genitive plural form (rosa-rum). What really distinguish a noun of the first declension is the ending in -ae, of the genitive singular. When you refer to a latin noun, you have to name the nominative and genetive cases. Noun entries in a vocabulary, are given in three parts:

puella, puellae, f.

The first part indicates the nominative singular, the second part the genitive singular and the third the gender of the noun. By removing the genitive ending, the stem of the noun is obtained (it is advisable to use the genetive because the stem of nominative can be different from the rest of the declension).

A noun of the first declension is declined by adding the following endings to the stem:

Case Singular Plural
Nominative -a -ae
Genitive -ae -arum
Dative -ae -īs
Accusative -am -ās
vecative -a -ae
Ablative -ā -īs

As an example the noun rosa, rosae, f. "The rose", is declined this way:

Singular:

Case Function
Nominative ros-ă The Rose, a Rose
Genitive ros-ae of the Rose
Dative ros-ae to/for the Rose
Accusative ros-ăm the Rose
Vocative ros-ă oh, Rose!
Ablative ros-ā with/by/from/etc. the rose

Plural:
Case Function
Nominative ros-ae Roses
Genitive ros-ārŭm of Roses
Dative ros-īs to/for Roses
Accusative ros-ās the Roses
Vocative ros-ae Oh, Roses!"
Ablative ros-īs with/by/from/etc. roses

The stem of rosa, rosae, f, is "ros". The difference between the nominative and vecative singular and ablative singular is in the pronunciation. The ablative singular ends with "ā", wich is a long syllable whilst in the nomivative there is "ă", a short syllable. By the context is possible to distinguish between the nominative and vecative singular as swell as for the other equal form.

Most nouns of the first declension are feminine, but there are a few which are masculine. An example is poëta, poëtae, m.:

Case Singular Plural
Nominative poët-ă poët-ae
Genitive poët-ae poët-ārum
Dative poët-ae poët-īs
Accusative poët-ăm poët-ās
vecative poët-ă poët-ae
Ablative poët-ā poët-īs

The diaeresis: "ë" indicates that oë is not a dipthong.
