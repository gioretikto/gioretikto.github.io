Indefinite negative pronouns

The indefinite pronouns found in negative sentences are: nemo, nihil, nullus, neuter.

Nemo

nemo means «nobody» (from ne-homo) is a masculine pronoun referred only to people; there is only the singular and it is seldomly used as adjective (nemo poëta = nullus poëta):

  • Nemo hoc cognoscit - «Nobody knows this»;

  • Nemo venit - «Nobody came»

  • Nemo non cotidie et consilium mutat et votum - «Everyone changes his plans and prayers day by day»;

  • nihil means «nothing», «nulla», «anything»; It is the neuter pronoun that corresponds to nemo; It is only used in the sing. nom. and acc.; it is replaced in oblique cases by the adjective nullus, which agrees in the feminine with res.

    • Nihil sine magno labore vita dedit mortalibus (Or.) - «Life grants nothing to men without hard work and efforts»

    • Nihil inimicius quam sibi ipse (Cic.) - «Man is his worst enemy»

    • Nihili est dictu facilius (Ter.) - «Nothing is esasier than to talk»

    nullus, nulla, nullum means «no one», «no», «not any» (from ne-ullus); it is the adjective which corresponds to the pronouns nemo and nihil (gen. nullīus, dat. nulli); it supplies nemo in its missing forms (gen. nullīus, abl. nullo) and in oblique cases it replaces nihil in agreement with res.

    • res Nullius - «Nobody's property».

    • Nullus miles fortior te est. - «No soldier is stronger than you».

    • Nullus dolor est quem non longiquitas temportis minuat ac molliat - «There is no grief which time does not lessen and soften».

    It is to be observed that rarely nullius and nullo serve as a substitute for the genitive and ablative of nihil:

    • Deus nullo magis hominem separavit a ceteris animalibus qvam dicendi facultate (Quinct.) - «God, that all-powerful Creator of nature and Architect of the world, has impressed man with no character so proper to disguish him from other animals, as by the faculty of speech»

    neuter, neutra, neutrum means «neither of», «nessuno dei due», used as a pronoun and adjective;. When followed up by a prnouns, takes the genitive: neuter nostrum, «neither of us»; when followed up by a noun sostantivo, si ha la concordanza: neuter poeta, «neither of the poets». It is also used in the plural, either with pluralia tantum or when referred to two groups.

    • Ubi Neutri transeundi initium faciunt. (Caes.) «As neither party chose to attempt a passage».

    • Duos qui sequitur lepores neutrum capit - «He who chases two hares catches neither».
