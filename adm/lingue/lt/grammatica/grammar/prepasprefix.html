Prepositions as prefixes

Prepositions are often prefixed to other words, particularly to verbs and adjectives, modifying their meaning.

A, ab, abs, "from", as duco, "I lead"; abduco, "I lead away" or "from"; It can also denote privation as "amnes", "mad".

Ad, "to"; as "adduco", "I lead to". It is sometimes intensive as adamo, "I love greatly".

De, "downward" as descendo, "I go down"; decido, "I fall down". Other times it is intensive: deamo, "I love greatly", or also privation despero ,"I despair", demens, "mad".

E or ex, "out of" from" , as exeo, "I go out". It is sometimes intensive as "exōro", "I beg earnestly", sometimes privative as exsanguis, "pale"; exspes, "hopeless".

In, signifies "into", "in" as in infero, "I bring in", irruo, "I rush against or upon".

With adjectives it reverses the signification as in infĭdus, "unfaithful"; indignus, "unworthy".

Per, "through" is commonly intensive, especially with adjectives, as perfacilis, "very easy". With quam it is strongly intensive as perquam facilis , "exceedingly easy". In perfidus,"perfidious", it is negative.

Prae, "before" with adjectives is intensive as praeclārus, "very famous", "very renowed".

Pro, denotes "forth", as produco, "I lead forth".

Sub, often diminishes the signification as rīdeo, "I laugh"; subrīdeo, "I smile"; albus, "white"; subalbus, "whitish". Sometimes denotes motion upwards as subrigo, "I raise up". Other times concealment as rapio, "I take"; subripio, "I take secretly", "I steal".
