Peculiarities of The Fourth Declension

The Fourth declension presents some peculiarities in case-forms:

In the genitive singular some nouns, next to the -us ending of the fourth declension, can have also the -i ending from the second declension:

Noun Meaning Genitive Singular
exercitus, m. army exercit-us / exercit-i
tumultus, m. il tumulto tumult-us / tumult-i
senatus, m. sanate senat-us / senat-i

In the dative and ablative plural end in -ŭbus and not in -ĭbus twi syllables nouns ending in the nominative in -cus, like the following:

Noun Meaning Dative and Ablative plural
arcus, -us, m. arc arc-ŭbus
acus, -us, f. needle ac-ŭbus
lacus, -us, m. lake lac-ŭbus
quercus, -us, f. oak querc-ŭbus
specus, -us, m. cave spec-ŭbus
tribus, -us, f. tribe trib-ŭbus
partus, -us, m. childbirth part-ŭbus
artus, -us, m. joint, limb art-ŭbus

Nouns as portus, -us, m., «port» and veru, -us, n., «spit» can end as portĭbus and portŭbus, verĭbus and verŭbus. Present a particular declension the nouns domus, f., «house», and Iesus, m., «Jesus». Domus has a mixed declension with forms belonging to the second declension

Case Singular Plural
Nominative dom-ŭs dom-ūs
Genitive dom-ūs dom-ŭŭm
Dative dom-uī (raro dom-ō) dom-ĭbŭs
Accusative dom-ŭm dom-ōs (raro dom-ūs)
Vocative dom-ŭs dom-ūs
Ablative dom-ō (raro dom-ū) dom-ĭbŭs

Domus present also the locative case domi, which means («at home», «in homeland», «in peace»); The locative case is an old latin case later absorbed by the ablative case. The noun domi can be joined to a possessive adjective denoting the possessor, or the genitive of the possessor; hence domi meae, (tuae, suae, nostrae, vestrae, alienae)

  • Manĕo domi meae - I stay at home.

  • Sum domi Bruti - I am at Brutu's house.

As we already stated, the accusative case, in this case domum is used to express motion towards a place («to home», ):

Redĕo domum meam - I go to my house.

The ablative case domo is used to express movement from a place:

Discēdo domo mea - I leave from my house.

The following sentences are frequenlty used:

Domi bellique, Domi militiaque, Domi et belli, vel belli vel domi - In peace and war.

Case Singular

Genitive Iēsu
Dative Iēsu
Accusative Iēsum
Vocative Iēsu
Ablative Iēsu
