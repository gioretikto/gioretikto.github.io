Chrysippus

Chrysippus of Soli (Greek: Χρύσιππος ὁ Σολεύς, Chrysippos ho Soleus; c. 279 – c. 206 BC), one of the greatest of the Stoics, was born probably in 280 B.C, at Soli in Cilicia. He attended the lectures, possibly of Zeno, and certainly of Cleanthes. The latter he succeded as recognized chief of the school.
