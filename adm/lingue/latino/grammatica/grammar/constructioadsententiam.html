Constructio ad sententiam

The Constructio ad sententiam is a type of construction in which the predicate does not agree with the grammatical subject but with what it is indicating, or representing: the logical subject of the proposition. This construction occurs with:

  • collective nouns: nouns denoting a multitude as multitudo, turba, pars, classis, exercitus etc.;

    • Multitudo hostium capti sunt - A multitude of enemies were captured (was captured).

      The grammatical subject is multitudo (sing. f.), but the predicate agrees in gender and number, with the logical subject hostes (plur. m.).

  • neuter substantives as milia, capĭta, servitia, or sometimes feminine, as furia, used in a figurative sense;

    • Capĭta coniurationis proscripti sunt - The leaders of the conspiracy were banned

      The grammatical subject is capita (plur. n.), but the predicate agrees with a plural masculine logical subject i.e. “the men” leading the conspiracy).

  • A pronoun as subject representing a multitude as uter, uterque, quisque etc, or adverbs like partim, along with either a genitive, or a noun followed by an ablative of accompaniment.

    • Uterque eorum exercitum edūcunt (Caes.) - Each of them draws out the army from the camp

    • Ipse dux cum aliquot principibus capiuntur (Liv. xxi. 60) - The general himself was captured with some leading men.
