Internal Accusative

Internal and External Accusatives

The External Accusative is the direct object of a transitive verb; An Intransitive Verb admits no direct object, but can still have an Internal Accusative that repeats the notion already contained in the root of the verb. Such is the case with verbs as: somniare somnium, "dream a dream", vitam vivere, "live a life", pugnare pugnam, "fight a fight", currere cursum, iurare iusiurandum, servire servitutem etc.

  • Nestor, tertiam iam aetatem hominum vivebat (Cic) - Nestor had already seen (lived) three human generations

  • Mirum somniavi somnium (Plaut.) - I have dreamed a wonderful dream

  • Vide ne facinus facias (Cic.) - Be careful not to commit any wrongdoing

  • Ego vestros patres ... vivere arbitror et eam quidem vitam, quae sola est nominanda" (Cic.) - I judge that your fathers are still alive, and they’ve been living the only life worthy of being called life
