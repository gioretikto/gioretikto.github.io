Aurum, (gold)

A hiātus or yawning, occurs when two vowels, which in ordinary circumstances form a diphthong, are to be pronounced separately; in this occurence the second vowel is marked with (") ; that is, a diaeresis — as poëta, «a poet»; aër, «air; aerius, airy». Apart from these Latinized Greek words a hiātus occurs more frequently when a word ends in a vowel, and the next begins with one, which is usually avoided in verse by the former of the vowels being thrown out (elisio)

Quantity of Vowels

The Quantity of a vowel is the time occupied in pronouncing it. A vowel is either short or long, while a diphtong is always long.

A short vowel is marked by ˘, as, ǎmor (love).

A long vowel is marked by ⁻, as māter (mother).

This distinction can be particularly important because it can indicate the different semantic and morphological value of omographic terms as:

lĭber ("book", noun)

līber ("free", adjective)

mălum ("harm", noun)

mālum ("an apple tree", noun)

lătus ("side", noun)

lātus ("broad, wide", adjective)

vĕnit ("he comes")

vēnit ("he came")

The Quantity of the Syllable is important for the position of the accent and in versification.
