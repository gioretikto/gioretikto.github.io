Adjectives of two endings

Third declension Adjectives of two endings possess masculine and feminine nominative alike, the neuter different. They are declined as facilis (m and f), facile (n), «easy»:

  Singular Plural
Case Masculine/Fem Neuter Masculine/Fem Neuter
Nominative facĭlis facĭlĕ facĭlēs facĭliă
Genitive facĭlĭs facĭlĭs facĭlĭŭm facĭlĭŭm
Dative facĭlī facĭlī facĭlĭbŭs facĭlĭbŭs
Accusative facĭlĕm facĭlĕ facĭlēs facĭliă
Vocative facĭlis facĭlĕ facĭlēs facĭliă
Ablative facĭlī facĭlī facĭlĭbŭs facĭlĭbŭs

Other adjectives with two endings are:

  • brevis, breve, «short»:

  • difficilis, difficile, «hard»

  • nobilis, nobile, «noble»

  • utilis, utile, «useful»

Follow this paradigm also the month names Aprilis, «april»; Quintilis, «july» and Sextilis, «agust». e.g.: Kalendis Sextilibus, «(1st day of Agust)».
