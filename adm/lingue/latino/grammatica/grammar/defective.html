Defective Verbs

There are many verbs which are not used in all the tenses, numbers, and persons; They are called Defective Verbs (from deficio "to be absent"). They can be divided in the following groups:

  • verbs wanting all present tenses, that are only used in the preterit tenses, that is, in the perfect and the tenses formed therefrom:

    • coepi, coepisti, coeptum, coepisse, «I have begun»;

    • memĭni, meministi, (without supine), meminisse, «I remember.»;

    • odi, odisti, (without supine), odisse, «I hate»;

  • verbs wanting many tenses and persons:

    • aio, «I say», «say yes»;

    • inquam, «I say»;

    • fari, «to speak» (chielfy in poetry);

  • verbs which are only used in a very few isolated forms:

    • quaeso, «prego»;

    • ave, «hail!»;

    • salve, «hail!»;

    • vale, «sta’ bene»;

    • cedo, «give me», «tell me».

    • apage, «begone!»; (the only form)
