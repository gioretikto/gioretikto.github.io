Ablative of Material

The material of which anything is made is expressed by the Ablative with the preposition e or ex, known as ablative of material:

  • Naves totae factae erant ex robore. (Caes.) - The ships were wholly made of oak

  • Statua ex euro, ex aere, facta - A statue made of gold, of bronze.

  • Universa Graecia in templo Delphici Apollinis statuam solido ex auro posuit - The whole of Greece set up his statue of solid gold in the temple of Delphic Apollo.

It is also possible a construction by means of adjectives used attributively:

  • Patĕra aurea - A golden cup

  • Signum marmoreum- A statue of marble
