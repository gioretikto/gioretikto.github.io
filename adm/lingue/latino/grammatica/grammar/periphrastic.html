Passive Periphrastic Conjugation

The Passive Periphrastic conjugation is a construction uesd to indicate necessity or obbligation, that combines the Gerundive plus the various tenses of sum. The are two passive Periphrastic constructions: personal and impersonal.

Personal Construction

The Passive Periphrastic Conjugation of Transitive verbs (also deponents) generally takes the personal construction. In this construction the Latin sentence has a subject expressed to which the gerundive, and the voice of the verb sum agree. An eventual agent in the sentence is indicated by the Dative of Agent.

  • Diligentia in omnibus rebus est nobis adhibenda. (Cic., De Orat.,) - We must use diligence in all things

  • Omnia quae secundum Naturam fiunt sunt habenda in bonis (Cic.) - All things that are done according to nature, are to be accounted good

Impersonal Construction

The Passive Periphrastic Conjugation is often Impersonal, i.e. no person is expressed in association with the necessary action. This construction employs an instransitive verb or a transitive verb without a direct object; It has the following characteristics:

  • The subject of the Latin sentence is not expressed

  • The gerundive is neuter singular (in -dum).

  • The form of the verb sum is in the third person singular.

Parse the following examples:

  • Pugnandum erat - One had to fight

  • Tibi non modo deliberandum est, verum etiam agendum (Cic.) - You must not only deliberate, but also act.

  • Seminumidam et Semigaetulum, non video quid mihi sit in ea re pudendum. (Apul.) - That I am half-Numidian and half-Gaetulian. I don’t see why this fact should cause me to be ashamed,

  • Si vis me flere, dolendum est primum ipsi tibi (Cic.) - If you would have me weep, you must first feel grief yourself.
