The Superlative

The superlative is formed by adding the termination -issĭmus, a, um, to the stem of the adjective:

Superlative strengthened

The superlative is strengthened by the adverb longē, "by far", facile, "without doubt" multo "much", vel, "even":

  • Ex Britannis omnibus longē sunt humanissimi qui Cantium incolunt - «By far the most cultivated of all Britons are those that inhabit the Kent.»

  • Plato fuit totius Graeciae facile doctissimus (Cicerone)

  • Protagoras sophistes illis temporibus vel maximus - «Protagoras, the very greatest sophist at that time»

  • Alcibiades omnium aetatis suae multo formosissimus fuit - «Alcibiades was by far the most handsome of all person of his age»

with unus or unus omnium, ("one above all others"):

  • Terentiam unam omnium aerumnosissimam sustenta (Cicerone)- Support Terentiam, who is by far the most unhappy.

  • Urbem unam mihi amicissimam declinavi (Caesar) - «I turned aside from a city above all others friendly to me».

  • P. Scaevolam unum nostre civitatis et ingenio et iustitia praestantissimum audeo dicere (Cic.)- I venture to call P. Scaevola by far the most distringuished man in our state both in ability and justice»

  • Militiades et antiquitate generis et gloria maiorum unus omnium maxime florebat (Nep.) - «Militiades was distinguished above all others both by the aniquity of his family and the glory of his ancestors»

with quam along with the verb possum:

  • Quam diligentissime - Ad diligenlty as possible

  • Unun omnium diligentissimus - «The one most diligent of all»

  • Quam aequissimo potest loco Caesar castra communit (Caesar) - «Caesar entrenches his camp in the most favourable position to be found».

  • .., Naves, in Venetiam, ubi Caesarem primum esse bellum gesturum constabat, quam plurimas possunt, cogunt. (Caes) - «They ssembled as many ships as possible in Venetia, where it was known that Caesar would begin the campaign».

A superlative is often joined with the pronoun quisque, which gives to it the general meaning - «All the people of belonging to a certain group", "as:

  • Săpientissĭmus quisque aequissimo animo moritur (Cic.)- «All the wisest of men die with the most resignation».

  • Altissima quaeque flumina minimo sono labuntur (Curt.)- « (All) the deepest rivers flow with the least noise».

    • Partitive genitive

    The superlative is often linked with a partitive genitive:

    Excellentissimi fuerunt Persarum Cyrus et Darius (Nep.) - The moust famous persians were Cyrus et Darius.

    whithout such a genitive, the superlative denotes a very high degree and it is called absolute superlative:

    • Faillimum vectigal est et honestissimum parsimonia . (Sen.) -

    • Nero scelleratissimus omnibus hominum fuit - Nero was the most wicked of all men.

    The partitive genitive can be introduced by the preposition e, ex and the ablative:

    • Quid ergo? audacissimus ego ex omnibus? Minime. (Cic.)

      - «What then? Am I the boldest of all these men? By no means.»

    or with inter or apud with the accusative:

    • Sextus Roscius, honestissimum inter suos - «Sextus Roscius was the most honest among his countrymen.»

    • Homerus Hesiodusque antiquissimi inter poetas Graecos sunt - «Homer and Hesiod are the oldest of Greek poets»

    • Apud Helvetios longe nobilissimus fuit et ditissimus Orgetŏrix. (Caes.) - «Among the Helvetians the noblest by far and the most wealthy was Orgetorix»
