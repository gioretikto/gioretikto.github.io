The Latin Alphabet

The Latin alphabet evolved from the Greek Alphabet, which was itself descended from the Phoenician alphabet. By the time of the Late Roman Republic (i.e. in the 1st c. BC) the Latin alphabet consisted of 23 letters (litterae):

In Classical Latin, there was no sound "v". The orthographic capital V (u if small) was used to represent the vowel /u/ sound as in VNUS (unus), as well as the consonantal /w/ sound as in VIVERE (uiuere).
