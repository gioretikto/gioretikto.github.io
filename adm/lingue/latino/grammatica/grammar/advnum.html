Numeral Adverbs

Numeral Adverbs denote the number of times that anything happens or is done, answering the question Quotiens? (Quoties?),«How many times?», «How often?», as:

  • semel in anno, «once a year»

  • bis in die, «twice a day»

The most used numeral adverbs are:

semel once

octiēs eight times

bis twice

noviēs nine times

ter three times

deciēs ten times

quater four times

vīciēs twenty times

quinquiēs five times

centiēs one hundred times

sexiēs six times

mīliēs one thousand times

septiēs seven times

Osservation

Are also Numeral Adverbs:

  • primum, «for the first time»;

  • itĕrum, «for the second time»;

  • tertium, «for the third time»

Nmeral Adverbs are used with mīlle to express the highet numbers, and almost exclusively in reckoning money; centēna mīlia is regularly omitted:

  • quăter et triciens (centēna mīlia) sestertium, «4,400,000 sesterces (four and thirty times a hundred thousand sesterces)».

  • vīcies ac novies mīlies (centēna mīlia), «2,900,000,000 sesterces (twenty-nine thousand times a hundred thousand sesterces)»

The neuter forms of ordinals can also be used as numeral adverbs: primum (for the first time), iterum for the second time, tertium, for the third time.

It is important to note the difference between the form like quăter e quărtum: quăter is the numeral adverb which indicates “How many times” anything happens, in this case «four times»; quărtum is neuter singular of the ordinal, with avverbial value, which means «for the fourth time»; sometimes, however, it is also possible to find also quăter meaning: «for the fourth time».
