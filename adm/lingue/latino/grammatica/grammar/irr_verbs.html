Irregular Verbs

Several verbs retain older form in the tenses of the present stem, or ocmbine two root in their inflection. Their are called irregular or anomalous verbs; They are:

  • sum, «I am», and its compounds, including possum;

  • fero, «I bring, carry, bear, endure», and its compounds;

  • volo, «I want», and the compounds nolo, «I don't want», and malo, «I prefer»;

  • eo, «I go», and its compounds, included queo, «potere», e nequeo, «non potere»;

  • fio, «I become, am made», and its compounds;

  • edo, «I eat», and its compounds.

The most common kind of anomaly is the presence of athematic endings in tenses derived from the present.

Thematic and athematic verbs

As ancient Greek, Latin as well has two kind of conjugations:

  • A Thematic conjugation in which the Person-endings are attached to the Verb-Stem by a vowel, called Thematic Vowel; e.g. lego, leg-i-s, leg-i-t, etc., has the Thematic vowel i.

  • An Athematic conjugation in which the Person-endings are attached to the Verb-Stem without any connecting vowel. E.g. fero, fer-s, fer-t, etc.

In latin the I, II, IV conjugations are Athematic; The desinences are attached direclty to the Stem-Verb ending in vowel. Verbs of the III conjugation with stems ending in consonant, show qualitative apophony with the stem vowel that can be either e or o (as in Greek). Vowel mutations and meetings have caused the transition to i of the thematic vowel in a great number of verbs belononing to the III conjugation.
