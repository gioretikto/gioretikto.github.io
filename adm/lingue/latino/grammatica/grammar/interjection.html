Interjections

Interjections (from interecio «a throwing between») are indeclinable words, being sounds uttered to express some strong emotion of joy, grief, wonder surprire, etc.,. Interjections have no grammatical connectiion with the other words in a sentence and they are commonly followed by the exclamation point. In all languages they are pretty nearly the same.

Interjections can be divided in two groups: proper and improper or impure interjections.

Proper interjections have no grammatical meaning, but they represent vocal mimicry of natural sounds indicating a sudden sensation or feeling. They can express:

  • Joy, ā, hā, io, ō, evoe, euhoe

  • Surprise, O! en! ecce! papae! atat!

  • Pain, ā, hā, au, heu, eheu, ei, hei, io, «alas!»

  • Disgust

    hui, pō, pōh

  • Astonishment and surprise, ehem, o, pō, «ah, oh!»

  • Grief, vae (with dative), heu, eheu, hei

  • Disgust, ăha, pro

  • incitement, eia, heia, euge!, «well done!, bravo!»

  • Silence, st

  • Calling attention, heus, ohe

Improper interjections

In Latin, it often happens that words belonging to other parts of speech like nouns, adverbs and verbs are used as interjections for exclamation purposes; these are known impoper interjections:

age, agĕdum agĭte come!, quick!

cedo!, "give up!"

sodes, "my good friend"

malum, nefas, infandum, shame shame!, wretched!

vivat! (vivant!), bene!, pulchre!

ave! (avete!), salve! (salvēte!), bene quiescas! (quiescatis!) salve!, addio!, buona notte!

hercŭle, hercle, mehercŭles, mehercŭle, mehercle, "By Hercules!"

Belle!, excellent!, bravo!

Bene!, very well!

cito!, quick!

All kinds of invocations of the gods may be regarded as interjections, as:

ecastor, mecastor per Castore!

edĕpol, "by Pollūx"

medius Fidius, me Dius Fidius, mediusfidius, «So may the God of faith help me!»
