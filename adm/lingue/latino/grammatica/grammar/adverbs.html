The Adverbs

Adverbs are intimate related to adjectives. While the latter modify nouns, adverbs modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs.

The positive degree of an adverb is formed in most cases from the positive degree of the corresponding adjective; In case of a first/second declension adjective, the adverb is formed by replacing the genitive singular desinence -i with -ē:

Adjective Genitive Adverb
liber («free») liber-i liber-ē («freely»)
miser («miserable») miser-i miser-ē («miserably»)
strenuus («brave») strenu-i strenu-ē «bravely»)

In case of a third declension adjective, the adverb is formed by replacing the desinence -is of the genitive singular with -ĭter;

Adjective Genitive Adverb
celer («quick») celer-is celer-ĭter («quickly»)
gravis («heavy») grav-is grav-ĭter («heavily»)
velox («fast») veloc-is veloc-ĭter («rapidly»)

Whend the 3rd declension adjective ends in either -ans, -antis or in -ens, entis, the desinence -is of the genitive singular is replaced with the -er.:

Adjective Genitive Adverb
const-ans («constant») constant-is constant-er («constantly»)
frequ-ens («frequent») frequent-is frequent-er («frequently»)

Comparatives and superlatives of adverbs

The comparative of majority of the adverb is equal to the neuter singular comparative ending in -ius (nom. and acc.) of the corrispondent adjective:

Positive Comparative
Adverb Adjective Adjective Adverb
alte («deeply») altus altior, altius altius
velocĭter («rapidly») velox velocior, velocius velocius
brevĭter («brefly») brevis brevior, brevius brevius
bene («well») bonus melior, melius melius
male («badly») malus peior, peius peius

The superlative of the adverb, is formed from the genitive singular of the corrispondent adjective by replacing the ending -i with th -ē.

POSITIVE SUPERLATIVE
Adverb Adjective Adjective Genitive Adverb
alte («deeply») altus altissimus altissim-i altissimē
elocĭter («rapidly») velox velocissimus velocissim-i velocissimē
brevĭter («briefly») brevis brevissimus brevissim-i brevissimē
bene («wll») bonus optimus optim-i optimē
male («bad») malus pessimus pessim-i pessimē

Peculiarities of comparatives and superlative of adverbs

We have seen that some adjectives form thecomparative and superlative by prefixing the adverbs magis and maxime to the positive degree. There are also adverbs which form the comparative and superlative in analogous way:

Positive Comparative Superlative
ardue magis ardue maxime ardue
strēnue magis strēnue maxime strēnue
idōneē magis idōneē maxime idōneē
necessarĭus magis necessarĭus maxime necessarĭus
