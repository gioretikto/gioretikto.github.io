Word Order

Unlike English, Latin has no definite or indefinite articles, and this allows a greater freedom in word order and juxtaposition of words. It is the same to say: Magister discipulum laudat or Discipulum laudat magister. In an inflected language, meaning depends on word endings; the -er and -um endings of the nouns in the previous sentences are sufficient to designate the subject and the direct object, thus the word order can be flexible.

In a latin sentence the most emphatic place is the first and second in importance is the last. Generally the subject is the most important word, and is placed firts with the verb which is the next in importance placed last.
