Substantivized adjectives

Sometimes adjectives can function as substantives; for this reason they are called Substantivized adjectives. In these constructions an adjective is used alone, and some substantive or other (pronouns) can be easily understood in terms of an ellipse, as in:

  • dextra, laeva (ellipsed. manus, pars), «the right, the left»

  • frigida, gelida, calida (ellipsed. aqua), «the cold, the warm»

  • primae, secundae (ellipsed. partes), «the first, the second»

  • birēmis, trirēmis (ellipsed. navis), «(nautical) the bireme/trireme»

  • hiberna, aestiva, stativa (ellipsed. castra), «the winter/summer, the permanent camp»

This construction occurs also with adjectives or participles, mostly plurals masculine, used to indicate a class of people:

  • divites, «i ricchi»;

  • pauperes, «i poveri»;

  • honesti, «gli onesti»;

  • docti, «i dotti»;

  • sapientes, «i sapienti»;

Some neuter Adjectives put abstraclty, generally in direct cases (either singular or plural) can also perform the office of substantives; in this case the adjective can also mean res, "thing".
