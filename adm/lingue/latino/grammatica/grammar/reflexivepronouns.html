Riflexive personal pronouns

Reflexive pronouns "reflect" the subject of the verb; i.e they refer to the subject; They are used when the subject of the verb is described as acting on himself, or as saying or thinking something about himself, as in:

  • Bestis homine uti possunt ad suam utilitatem (Cic.)- Men can make use of animal for their own advantage

    • The Personal Pronouns for the 1st and 2nd person can be used either with or without reflexive meaning. The meaning is reflexive when the personal pronoun refers back to the subject of the sentence or clause. It is non-reflexive when it refers back to someone other than the subject of the sentence or caluse.

    (Ego) me laudo

    I praise myself- (reflexive)

    (Tu) te laudas.

    you praise yourself- (reflexive)

    (Ego) te laudo.

    I praise you -(not reflexive)

    (Tu) me laudas.

    you praise me -(not reflexive)

    Te video.

    I see you - (not reflexive)

    Me video.

    I see myself (reflexive)

    The reflexive pronouns for the 3rd person sui, sibi, se, singular and plural, have only reflexive sense. For this they are only used when referring to the subject of a sentence:

    • Brutus se monet - «Brutus reproaches himself»

    • Et abiens laqueo se suspendit - «And going away he hanged himself»
