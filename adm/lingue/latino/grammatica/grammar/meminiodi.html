Memĭni and odi

Memĭni and odi have in the perfect, pluperfect and future perfect the sense of the present, imperfect and future: the indicate in the present the consequence of a past action; This charateristics is known as the 'logical perfect' ('perfectum logicum');

memĭni (from the root of mens) means litterarly «I am mindful», thus «I remember»;

odi means litterarly «I have conceived abhorrence», thus «I hate»; They are conjugated as follows:

Parse the meaminings: if the perfect has value of present (memĭni, «I remember»), pluperfect has value of imperfect (meminĕram, «I remembered») and future perfect has value of future (meminĕro, «I will remember»); analogamente: odi, «io odio»; odĕram, «io odiavo»; odĕro, «io odierò».

It is also a logical perfect, though, not defective (it has the present forms), the verb novi, novīsti, novīsse, meaning «I've known», thus «I know». The present forms of novi are: nosco, noscis, noscĕre.
