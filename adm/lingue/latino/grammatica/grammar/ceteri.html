Indeterminate pronouns alii, relĭqui, cetĕri

The expressions "others", "the others", "the other things" in Latin are rendered by different indeterminate pronouns and adjectives.

Alii, aliae, alia

Alii means «some others (things or persons)» in opposition to one or several:

  • Aliorum opiniones praeteribo nam enumerare illas longum est - «I will omit the opinions of others that it would be long to enumerate»

cetĕri, cetĕrae, cetĕra

ceteri means «the other, remainder, rest». The presence of the suffix -ter gives the idea of the comparison between the members of one group to that of another.

  • Hannibal tanto praestitit ceteros imperatores prudentia quanto populus Romanus antecedat (Nep.) - «Hannibal excelled other commanders in prudentia by as much as the Roman pepole exceed al nations in bravey»

  • Ubii paulo quam ceteri humaniores sunt - «Ubi are a little more civil than others»

relĭqui, relĭquae, relĭqua

relĭqui, relĭquae, relĭqua means "the others", "the rest", referring to something definite but "residual", often in number or reduced quantity compared to the total; is used especially in numerical expressions, as "the rest" (reliquiae) of a whole.
