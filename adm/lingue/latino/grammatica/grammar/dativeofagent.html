Dative of Agent

The personal agent is generally expressed by the Ablative of Agent introduced by the preposition a, or ab. With the passive periphrastic, however, the personal agent is normally expressed by the dative case without a preposition, construction known as Dative of Agent.

  • Caesari omnia uno tempore erant agenda – Caesar had to do everything at once

  • Mihi consilium captum iamdiu est (Cic.) - For me the decision was taken long ago

  • Castris est vobis utendum, non palaestra. (Nep.) - You must cultivate the exercises of the camp, not those of the gymnasium.

With the passive periphrastic, the ablative of agent can still be used, chiefly to avoid ambiguity resulting from the presence of another dative which could be confused with the dative of agent, e.g.

  • A me oboediendum est tibi - I must obey you

    Mihi oboediendum est tibi - Could be read as "I must obey you" or as "You must obey me"

  • senātuī a te persuadendum est - The Senate must be persuaded by you.

    senātuī te persuadendum est - Could be read as "The Senate must be persuaded by you" or as "You must be persuaded by the Senate"

However in a sentence there can be two datives where there isn't ambiguity:

  • Servis obediendum est domino - Slaves must obey their masters
