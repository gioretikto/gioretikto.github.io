The Irregular Verb fero

The verb fero, fers, tuli, latum, ferre, «To bring, carry, bear, endure» base its conjugation on three different stems for the present, perfect, and supine:

  • fĕr-, stem of the present

  • tŭl-, stem of the perfect, from an ancient perfect of tollo, te-tŭli

  • lā-, stem of the supin (from *tla)

The compounds of fero

The compounds of fero, are declined likewise:

  • au-fĕro, -fers, abstŭli, ablātum, auferre, «To take away, carry off,» (from ab + fero)

  • af-fĕro, -fers, attŭli, allātum, afferre, «To bring to, carry, convey» (from ad + fero)

  • antĕ-fĕro, -fers , antetŭli, antelātum, anteferre, «To carry in front, bring in advance» (pron. antèfero, àntefers, antètuli, antelàtum)

  • circum-fĕro, -fers, circumtŭli, circumlātum, circumferre, «To carry, bear or move around or about;»

    • (pron. circùmfero, circùmfers, circùmtuli)

  • con-fĕro, -fers, contŭli, collātum, conferre, «To bring or collect together, gather; unite, join» (from cum + fero)

  • de-fĕro, -fers, detŭli, delātum, deferre, «To bear, carry or bring down or away»

  • dif-fĕro, -fers, distŭli, dilātum, differre, «To carry away in different directions, disperse» (from dis + fero)

  • ef-fĕro, -fers, extŭli, elātum, efferre, «To bring forth, carry forth or out», «To carry out for burial» (from ex + fero)

  • in-fĕro, -fers, intŭli, illātum, inferre, «To bring or carry into»

  • of-fĕro, -fers, obtŭli, oblātum, offerre, «To offer, bring forward» (from ob + fero)

  • per-fĕro, -fers, pertŭli, perlātum, perferre, «To support, suffer, bear patiently»

  • prae-fĕro, -fers, praetŭli, praelātum, praeferre, «To prefer»

  • pro-fĕro, -fers, protŭli, prolātum, proferre, «To put forward, bring out»

  • re-fĕro, -fers, rettŭli, relātum, referre, «To bring or carry back or again»

  • trans-fĕro, -fers, transtŭli, translātum, transferre, «To transfer»

  • suf-fĕro, -fers, sustinui, sustentum, sufferre, «To bear, support, endure»
