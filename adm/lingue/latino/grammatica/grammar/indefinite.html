Indefinite adjectives and pronouns

Indefinite pronouns and adjectives are used in opposition to adjectives to refer to people or things without saying exactly who or what they are. In most cases they are composed by quis(qui) or uter and indeclinable suffixes and prefixes.

Indefinite pronouns can represent either count nouns or noncount nouns and include a number of sub-categories:

  • universal (such as everyone, everything)

  • assertive existential (such as somebody, something)

  • elective existential (such as anyone, anything),

  • negative (such as nobody, nothing).

Quis, quid

The pronoun quis,quid and adjective qui, quae (qua), quod are declined as the interrogative adjective. They are never found at the beginning of a sentence and they are moslty used after si, nisi, ne, num, sive, seu, alius etc., in hypothetical sentences:

  • Si quis dixerit.., «If anyone shall affirm ..»

  • Si quid habes certius, velim scire (Cic.) - «If you have something of more certain I want to know it».

  • Potest quis errare aliquando, «A person may err sometime»

The pronoun aliquis "someone" and the adjective aliqui, "some" are composed by ali- (reduced stem of alius) and by quis; they are opposed to quisquam (pron.) and ullus (adj.)."no one", "none", used in negative sentences.

  • Semper aliqui anquirendi sun quos diligamus - «We must always seek some persons to love».

  • Mitte mihi aliquam epistulam- «Send me some letter».

  • Mitte ad nos de tuis aliquem tabellarium (Cic.) - «Send us as courier one of yours».

Quidam

Quidam means "one, a, a certain one":

  • quidam imperator, «a certain emperor»
