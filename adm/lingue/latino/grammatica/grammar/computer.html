The compounds of uter

There are some indefinite compounds of uter used either as adjective of pronouns:

  • Uterque, utrăque, utrumque (pronoun and adjective) which means «each of two, both», . In its declension uter is declined, while the enclitic -que does not change, (gen. utriusque, dat. utrique, acc. utrumque, utramque, utrumque ecc.) There is also the plural: utrīque, utraeque, utrăque, used either with pluralia tantum or when the indicate groups of persons/things.

  • Utervis, utrăvis, utrumvis (pronoun and adjective) means «either of the two», litt. «whomever of the two you want». It is a compound of uter, which is declined, and -vis («vuoi», 2nd person of volo), whihc does not change: gen. utriūsvis, dat. utrīvis, acc. utrumvis, utramvis, utrumvis etc.

  • Uterlĭbet, utralĭbet, utrumlĭbet (pronoun and adjective) means «either of the two», litt. «whomever of the two you want». It is composed by uter, declinabile, and by -libet («to like») which is indeclinabile: gen. utriuslĭbet, dat. utrilĭbet, acc. utrumlĭbet, utramlĭbet, utrumlĭbet etc.
