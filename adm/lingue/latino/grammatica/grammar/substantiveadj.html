Special uses of adjectives

Like in English, in many sentences, adjectives are used to perform the purpose of nouns: the rich, the blind, the jobless, the unemployed, the deaf, the stupid ,etc. In latin also the adjective is capable of of being used as a noun, as in:

  • honestae, «The Honest women».

  • bona, «The good things.

  • boni, «The good men»

  • miseri, «The poor people».

  • Vera, «The true things».

  • Audentes Fortuna adiuvat, «Fortune comes to the aid of those daring»

  • Nostri, integris viribus, fortiter pugnabant, «Our men, having fresh forces, were fighting vigorously».

The neuter adjective is frequently used in direct cases (nom., acc., voc.), to imply the term "thing":

  • bonum, «The good thing», «The good»

  • verum (vera, plur), «The true thing», «The truth»
