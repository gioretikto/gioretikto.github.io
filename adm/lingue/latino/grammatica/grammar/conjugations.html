The conjugations

Latin Verbs are divided into four categories known as conjugations. A conjugation is a category of verbs that form their tenses in the same way. Conjugations consists in appending certain endings to the verbal stem. The conjugations are distinguished from one another by the form of the present infinitive active, as follows:

ExampleConjugation Infinitive Termination Distinguishing vowel
amāre I -āre ā long
monēre II -ēre ē long
legĕre III -ĕre ĕ short
audire IV -īre ī long

The vocabulary entry for latin verbs shows the principal parts of the verb. Principal parts of Latin verbs, give all the information from which the full conjugation of the verb may be derived. For example the verb "amo", "I love", appears as follows:

amo, amas, amāvi, amātum, amāre

where:

  • amo, "I love", first person singular, present active indicative

  • amas, "You love" second person singular, present active indicative

  • amavi, "I loved" first person singular, perfect active indicative

  • amatum, supine active in -um

  • amāre, "to love", present active infinitive
