Peculiarities of the Third Declension

Also in the third declension there are nouns which occur in the singular number only (Singularia tantum):

  • senectus, senectūtis, f., «old age»

  • sanguis, sanguĭnis, m., «blood»

  • piĕtas, piĕtātis, f., «piety»

  • plebs, plebis, f., «plebe, volgo, mob»

  • proles, prolis, f., «piety»

  • vesper, vespĕris, m., «evening»

which occur in the plural number only (Pluralia tantum):

Festivity nouns:

  • Ambarvalia, Ambarvalium, n.,

  • Bacchanalia, Bacchanalium, n.,

  • Lupercalia, Lupercalium, n.,

  • Saturnalia, Saturnalium, n.,

Gographical nouns or of cities:

  • Alpes, Alpium, f., «The Alps»

  • Sardes, Sardium, f., «Ancient capital city of Lydia»

  • Ciclădes, Ciclădeum, f., le Cicladi

  • Gades, Gadium, f., Cadice

  • other nouns:

    manes, manium, m., «the spirits of dead ancestors»

    moenia, moenium, n., «walls»

    penates, penatium, m., «The household gods»

    viscĕra, viscĕrum, n., «organs contained in the abdomen»

    • Certain nouns can have different meaning in the plural and singular:

    SingularMeaning PluralMeaning
    aedes, aedis, f., temple aedes, aedium, f., house
    carcer, carcĕris, m., prison carcĕres, -um, m., gates
    finis, finis, m., the end fines, finium, m., limit, border, boundary
    pars, partis, f., The part partes, partium, f., faction, party, part (of an actor)
    sors, sortis, f., lot, fate sortes, sortium, f., oracular response

    Nouns with irregular declensions

    There are nouns of the third declension with irregular case endings, like vis, roboris, sf., «force» which in the genitive and dative singular has the stem robor (from robur, robóris, «oak»):

    Case Singulare Plural
    Nominative vis vires
    Genitive robŏris virium
    Dative robŏri virĭbus
    Accusative vim vires
    Vocative vis vires
    Ablative vi virĭbus

    bos, bovis, m. and f - «a cow, bull, or ox», wich is regular in the singular but irregular in the plular:

    Case Singulare Plural
    Nominative bos boves
    Genitive bovis boum
    Dative bovi bubus, bobus
    Accusative bovem boves
    Vocative bos boves
    Ablative boves bubus, bobus

    other irregular nouns are:

    • iecur, iecŏris, n., «liver»

    • iter, itinĕris, n., «walk, street, passage, route, medium»;

    • iugĕrum, iugĕri, n., iùgero; «A juger, a Roman unit of area»

    • Iuppĭter, m.,«Jupiter, Jove»

    • sal, salis, sm and n., «salt»

    • supellex, supellectĭlis, sf., «furniture»

    • sus, sŭis, sm and f «pig»

    • vas, vāsis, sn, «vessel, dish»

    • caro, carnis, sf, «meat»

    Declension of Greek noun

    The vast majority of greeks nouns belonging to the third decleniosn follow the latin declension, but in some cases along with the latin case-endingd is also possible to find the greek ones. Some examples are:

    • Genitive singular ending in -os (along with the latin -is): Arcados, Pallados, Iliădos, Aeneĭdos.

    • Accusative singular ending in -in and -yn (along with the latin-im e -ym): Agamemnŏna, Pallăda, aëra, Zeuxin, Tethyn , etc.

    • Accusative plural ending in -as (along with the latib -es): Cyclădas, herōas, cratēras, lampădas, Macedŏnas, Lingŏnas, Allobrŏgas, Aethiŏpas.

    Feminine proper names such as Sappho, "Sappho"; Dido, «Dido» and Calypso, «Calypso» are indeclinable, except in the genitive for which we have Sapphus, Didus, Calypsus; Dido, has also the Latinized forms: Dido, Didis, Didum, Didum, Dido, Didone.
