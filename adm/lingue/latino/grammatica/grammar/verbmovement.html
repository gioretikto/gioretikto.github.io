Accusative for verbs of movement

The verbs indicating movement as eo, curro, venio, gradior, etc., are generally intransitive; when their a compounded with prepositions that take the accusative, as trans, circum, ob, praeter, etc., they become transitive and thus can have the passive voice in all forms (The only exceptions are deponents).

  • Iugurtha Auli castra circumvēnit. (Sall.) - Giugurta circondò l’accampamento di Aulo.

  • Tamĕsis aegre pedibus transiri potest. (Caes.) - Il Tamigi può essere attraversato a piedi con difficoltà.

  • Helvetii Caesarem in itinere convenerunt. (Caes.) - The Helvetians met with Caesar while he was marching.

  • Flumen Tamesis uno omnino loco pedibus transiri potest. (Caes.) - The Thames can be crossed on foot with difficulty.

Auli castra a Iugurtha circumventa sunt. L’accampamento di Aulo fu circondato da Giugurta.
