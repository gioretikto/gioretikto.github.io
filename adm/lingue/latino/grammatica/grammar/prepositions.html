Prepositions

A Preposition is an indeclinable part of speech placed before nouns, pronouns, adjectives or joined to an infinitive verb (from prae-pono, «I place in front of, or before.»), with the function of relating them. The primary relations are those of Place, Time and Number. The principal relations expressed by prepositions are of:

  • Motion towards: ad, erga, tenus, versus.

  • Motion from: a, ab, e, ex, de

  • Precedence: ante, prae, pro, super, supra

  • Posteriority: post, pone, infra, sub, subter, secundum

  • Union: apud, cum, coram, circum, propte, iuxta

  • Opposition: adversus, contra, ob

  • Inclusion: inter, intra

  • Exclusion: absque, sine, extra, clam

  • Transition: cis, citra, per, praeter, trans

Latin preposition can govern different cases and can be distinguished in:

  • Preposition that govern the Accusative

  • Preposition that govern the Ablative

  • Preposition that govern both the Accusative and the Ablative
