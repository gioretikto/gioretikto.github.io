The Irregular Verb edo

The verb edo, edis, edi, esum, edĕre, «I eat», follows regularly the paradigm of the 3rd conjugation. In the tenses of the present stem, though, in several of its forsm a syncope is sometimes employed, in consequence of which they become like the corresponding tenses of the verb sum; this phenomen occurs particularly in the form of sum beginning in es, as in: *ed-s > es; *ed-t > est; *ed-tis > estis etc.

Passive Voice

Edo is conjugated regularly in the passive voice and the syncope takes place only in edĭtur (3rd pers. sing. indic. pres.) and ederetur (3a pers. sing. sub. imperf.) whereby the athematic forms estur and essetur are formed.

The athematic forms of edo are different from those the verb sum, in respect of the quantity of the root vowel; Pay attention to the following forms:

  • ĕs with a short e derives from sum: «you are»;

  • ēs with a long e derives from edo: «you eat».

In like manner are conjugated the compounds of edo:

  • com-ĕdo, is, comēdi, comēsum, comedĕre, "I consume or devour"

  • ex-ĕdo, is, exēdi, exēsum, exedĕre, "I eat up, devour, consume"
