Distributive and reciprocal use of alter and alius

Alter, Alius, and their derived adverbs are is frequently paired in different cases to denote a reciprocal relationship (= "each other") or a distributive one.

Distributive

  • aliī alia dīcunt. - «Some people say some things, and others say other things»

  • alii alio more vivebant - «Some people had certain customs and some had others»

  • alius aliud fecit, or alii alia fecerunt - «Some did this, others that»

Reciprocal

  • alter alterīus manu ceciderunt - «Both fell, one by the hand of the other»

  • Alius alium percontamur - «We ask one another»

  • Fratres alter alterum hortantur- «The two brothers exhort each other»
