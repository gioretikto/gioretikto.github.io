Supine

The Supine is a verbal noun of the fourth declension with only two cases:

  1. The accusative ending in -um

  2. The Ablative ending in -ū

Supine in -um

The supine in -um is used after Verbs of motion to express purpose, and has an actvive voice, as:

  • Hannibal Carthaginem deefensum in Africam revocatus est - Hannibal was recalled in africa to defende Cathago

  • Legati ad Cesarem gratulātum convenerun - envoys came to Caesar to congratulate him

  • Cubĭtum discessĭmus. (Cic.) - we got to sleep

The Supine in -um may take an Object, as in:

  • Legati venerunt questum iniurias. (Liv.) - envoys came vennero a lamentarsi delle ingiurie

  • do filiam nuptūm - I give my daughter in marriage

The Supine in -ū

The Supine in -ū, is used as an Ablative of Specification along with the adjectives facilis, difficilis, utilis, inutilis, optimus, turpis, incredibilis, iucundus, optimus, dignus, indignus. It is traditionally know as "passive supine", and limited to few transitive verbs; the most common are: dictu, audītu, factu, inventu, memoratu, visu.

  • Quod optimum factu videbitur, facies - You'll do the most convinient thing.

  • O rem non modo visu foedam, sed etiam auditu! (Cic.) - what a shameful thing not only to be seen but also to be heard.

  • Incredibile memoratu est .. (Sall.) - Incredibile to say it.

  • Aspera perpressu funt iucunda relatu - Painful thinhgs to become joyful to remember

  • dignum memoratu (Liv.) - wortfull of be remembered

  • Ita dictu opus est. (Ter.) - So we must say.

The Supine in -u never takes an object.
