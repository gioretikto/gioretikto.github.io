Prepositions with the Ablative

A, ab «from, by»

It is mainly used to indicate origin and separation in relations of time as well as place. Ab is used chiefly before consonants and a before vowels including h.

  • Ab hora tertia bibebatur (Cic.) - They commenced drinking at the third hour

  • Ab his castris oppidum Remorum nomine Bibrax aberat milia passuum octo (Caes.) - From this camp a town of the Remi called Bibrax was eight miles distant.

  • A puero (Cic.) - From boyhood

  • Ab urbe condita - From the Founding of the City

  • Ab initio - from the beginning

to indicate the agent by or through which or whom a thing fis done or takes place;

  • Ego autem a patre ita eram deductus ad Scaevolam sumpta virili toga (Cic.) - I myself was led off by my father to Scaevola right after I took up the toga virilis

Cum «with, along with»

  • Cum primo lumine solis (Verg.) - With the sun's first light

  • How miserius; Hoc turpius quicquam? vagamur egentes cum coniugibus et liberis (Cic.) - Is there anything more wretched, more disgraceful than this, that we wander in want with wives and children?

  • Bellum cum Latinis gessit (Liv.) - He waged war with the Latins

  • Itaque rideo istos, qui turpe existimant cum servo suo cenare. (Sen.) - Therefore I laugh about those who think it is disgraceful to eat with one's slave

De «Down from, from, during, in the course of, made of»

of place

  • De caelo demissus - Sent down from heaven

  • Murus et porta de caelo tacta erant (Livy 32.29.1) - "The wall and the gate had benn struck by lightning".

  • De navibus desilire - to jump down from the ships

of time

  • De tertia vigĭla (Caes.) - In the course of the third watch

of manner:

  • De Gallis triumphare - to triumph for victories gained over the Gauls

  • De improviso - unexpectedly, suddenly

  • De industria, on purpose

  • De integro - anew

In the Ablative of Material:

  • Niveo factum de marmore signum (Ov.) - A statute made of snow-white marble

In the partitive ablative:

  • unus de multis (Cic.) - one of the many

  • Unus de plebe - on of the people

figuratively signifies concering, about

  • Missi sun legati de pace (Cic.)- Ambassadors were sent to enter into negociations for peace

  • De republica (scripti libri) (Cic.) - Books written on hte subject of the commonwealth

Ex, e «from, out of»

Of place

  • profugere ex urbe; (Caes) - To leave, quit, fiy out of the City;

Also figuratively:

  • ex periculo servare - to save from danger

Of time

  • Ex hoc die - from this day forth

To express origin or cause

  • Ex vulnere aeger (Cic.) - Weak through his wound

  • Ex renibus laborare (Cic.) - To be in pain in the kidneys

In the partitive ablative construction:

  • Quidam ex militibus (Caes.) - (A certain) one of the soldiers

In adverbial expresssions

  • ex industria - on purpose

  • ex improviso - unexpectedly

  • e contrario - on the contrary

  • ex ordine - according to order ; in order;

  • ex tempore - off hand;

  • ex omni parte - altogether

Prae, «Before, because of, with (in comparison)»

Of place, as

  • Tiberim prae se armentum agens nando traiecit (Liv.) - He swam across the Tiber, driving the herd before him

  • Prae Se ferre - to carry before one, to hold before one's self (hence figuratively) exhibit, proclaim

whend indicating the cause, Prae signifies because of and is found chiefly in negative sentences:

  • Prae lacrimis loqui non possum (Cic.) I cannot speak for tears

  • Solem prae iaculorum multitudine non videbitis (Cic.) - You will not see the sun for the number of darts

  • Nec loqui prae maerore potuit (Cic.) - And he could not speak for grief

Plautus uses prae also in the positive sense

  • Prae laetitia lacrimae praesiliunt mihi (Plaut.) - Tears start to my eyes for joy

  • Prae lassitudine opus est ut lavem (Plaut.) - I am so fatigued I must take a bath

In comparison signifies with, by the side of, as

  • Romam prae sua Capua inridebunt (Cic.) - They will laugh at Rome compared with their own Capua

Pro, «Before; in front of; on behalf of; in consideration of; in proportion to»

Pro denotes before of place as

  • Castra pro moenibus locata erant (Liv.) - The camp had been pitched in front of the walls

  • Aciem pro castris instruit - He drew up his army before the fort.

  • Laudati pro contione omnes sunt (Liv.) - They were all commended in front of the assembled army

With the notion of defending something, it signifies "on behalf of, in favour or" in expression as

  • Dimicare pro patria (Cic.) - to fight for one's country

  • Ego pro soldati et pro mea omni fama decerno (Cic.) - I am fighting the last battle for my friend and for my own character altogether

It signifies in place of, instead of, as

  • Scribam pro rege obstruncat - He kills the Secretary instead of the King

  • Lubenter verba iungebant, ut soles pro si audes, sis pro si vis (Cic.) - They were fond of joining words, as sodes for si audes, sis for si vis

It signifies, according to in proportion to, as

  • Pro re nata (Cic.) - According to circumstances

  • Proelium atrocius quam pro numero pugnantium (Liv.) - A battle more sanguinary than might have been expected from the number of the combatants

Sine, «without»

as the opposite of cum it signifies without

  • Homo sine spe - A man without hope

  • Ariovistus respondit neminem secum sine sua pernicie contendisse (Caes. Gall. l,36,6)- Ariovistus answered nobody had fought with himself save to his own destruction

  • Non sine dis (Hor.) - Not without the help of the gods

  • Nullus dies sine linea (Prov. in Plin.) - Not a day without a line

  • Ipsa Virtus amicitiam et gignit et continet, nec sine virtute amicitia esse ullo pacto potest. (Cic.) - But this virtue is the parent and preserver of friendship and without virtue friendship cannot exist at all

  • Nihil sine magno labore vita dedit mortalibus (Hor.) - «Life grants nothing to men without hard work and efforts»

Tenus, «Up to, as far as»

  • Antiochus Tauro tenus regnare iussus est (Cic.) - It was ordained that Antiochus should rule only as far as the Taurus

  • Verbo tenus - In word only

  • nomine tenus - by name / in name only
