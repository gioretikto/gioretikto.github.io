The Comparative of equality

The comparison of equality is expressed in English with "as much ... as" or "so .... as":

  • I eat as much as a lion

  • Martin is as tall as John

  • He likes ice cream as much as She does.

In latin it is formed in a similar way as:

  • "tam ... quam"

  • "ita .. ut",

  • "aeque ... ac"

  • sicut ... ita

The sentence "The dog is as fast as the hare" can be expressed in three equivalent forms:

Canis

est tam velox quam lepus
est ita velox ut lepus
est velox aeque ac lepus

Consider also the following examples are:

  • Romani minus eloquentes quam Graeci sunt- «Romans are less eloquent than Greeks»

  • Nihil est tam omnibus carum quam libertas - «Nothing is more dear to anybody than freedom»

  • Nihil est tam fallax quam vita humana, nihil tam insidiosum (Sen.) - «Nothing is so deceptive as human life, nothing is so treacherous».

  • Hebes est aeque ac pecus (Cic.) - «He's as stupid as a sheep»
