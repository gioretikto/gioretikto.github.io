Assimilation and dissimilation

Assimilation is a phonological phenomenon whereby one sound becomes more like a nearby sound. The assimilation occurs generally through consonant mutation. There are two types of Assimilation:

  • Regressive: if the second consonant assimilates the first - as in:

    • ad-teneo > attineo;

    • ob-caedo > occīdo;

    • ad-cedo > accēdo;

    • in-ludo > illūdo;

    • *sup-mos > summus;

    • *puer-la > puella;

    In the last cases the assimilation is total, because the first consonant became idential to the second. It is only partial, in

    • *srib-ii > scripsi, "I wrote"

    • *leg-tos > lect

    • *ag-tos > actus

  • Progressive: When the second consonant is assimilated in the first. This process occurs frequently when two similar sounds in succession may be difficult or unpleseant to pronounce thus the first is modified:

    • *tol-no > tollo;

    • *tor-seo > torreo;

    • *fer-se > ferre;

    • *vel-se > velle;

    • *mol-duis. > mollis

The Assimilation process occurs also in the meeting between nasal and mute consonants (labials and dentals). The m becomes n before dentals, e.g.: quemdam, quamdam, quorumdam become quendam, quandam, quorundam. Conversely, dental n before the labials p and b became labial m:

  • in-berbis > imberbis;

  • in-par > impar;

  • *op-nis > omnis (cf. opes);

  • in-patiens > impatiens;

  • *sop-nus > somnus, "sleep", (cf. sopor)

before either l or m, total assimilation occurs:

  • in-laesus > illaesus;

  • in-libatus > illibatus;

  • in-lustris > illustris;

  • in-maturus > immaturus;

  • in-mortalis > immortalis;

  • in-motus > immotus;

The assimilation can also involve vowels: homo derives from *hemo (cf. nemo, derived from ne-hemo); momordi is the doubled perfect of mordeo, but the ancient form is memordi.

Dissimilation is the opposite process, in which sounds become more unlike neighboring:

  • *medidies > meridies, "midday"

  • *caeluleus (cf. caelum) > caeruleus

  • *canmen > carmen

Dissimilation often involves loss of sounds as in:

  • *nutri-trix > nutrix (cf. genetrix);
