Interrogative qualis? quantus? quot? quotus?

Other interrogative adjectives and pronouns are:

  • qualis?, quale?, «of What Kind?», «of What sort?»; used to ask the “quality” it is declined as a first/second declension adjective; ha lo stesso valore di qui?, quae?, quod?, ma è usato meno frequentemente;

    • Qualis fuit Hercoles?, «Fortis et magnanimus»

  • quantus?, quanta?, quantum?, «How big?», «How great?»; it is used to ask the “magnitude”, but never the “quantity”, it is declined as a first/second declension adjective;

    • quantus Consistit sumptus? (Or.) -«How great is the expense?»

    • quanta spe? -«How big the hope?»

  • quot?, «How many?»; it is indecinable and used to ask the “quantity”; in the pluaral is used as an adjective and agree in case with the nouns it refers to:

    • Quot hominibus hoc donum dedit? «Did you give this gift to How many men?».

    • Quot homines, tot sententiae (Cic.), «There are as many opinions as there are men».

    Se il “quanti?” ha valore di sostantivo, si rende con quam multi? (non quot). Es.: Quam multi contioni interfuerunt? «Quanti parteciparono all’assemblea?»;

  • quotus?, quota?, quotum?, «What number of?», «How many?», «Which in the series?»; The question asked by Quotus is answered by an ordinal:

    • Quota hora est? – Quarta. «Which is the hour (of the day)? – The fourth» (i.e. 10 AM)

    • Quot fuerunt Caesaris et Cleopatrae filii? Unus, Caesarion, -«How many children Did Caesar and Cleo have? One, Caesarion»
