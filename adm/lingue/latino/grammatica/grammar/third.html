Third Declension

The third declension is the largest and contains masculine, feminine and neuter nouns. There are so many different possible nominative forms for third declension nouns, but they all end in is in the genitive singular, for example:

rex, rēgis, m., «king»:

caput, capitis, n., «head»

Third Declension nouns are categorized in two major classes:

  • Imparisyliaba (which means unequal in the number of their syllables): They have more syllables in the genitive than in the Nominative singular. For example:

    con-sul, con-su-lis, m., «consul»

    la-us, la-ud-ĭs, f., «praise»

    cor-pus, cor-pŏ-ris, n., «body».

  • Parisyllaba, having the same number of syllables in the nominative and genitive singular.

    • pa-ter, pat-ris, m., «father»

    ma-ter, mat-ris, f., «mother»

    na-vis, navis, f. «ship»

For the sake of organization, we divide thrid declension nouns in three groups, differing in the ending of the ablative singular, genitive plural, and of direct cases, of the neuter plural:

  • I gorup: Imparisyliaba nouns of any gender which have only one consonant before the desinence -is. Some example:

    • con-sul, con-sŭ-lis,

    labor, laboris, m. «work, labour»

    dolor, doloris, m., «pain»

    Nouns of this group end in the abl. sing. in -e, and in the gen. plur. in -um; neuter nouns in the nom., acc., voc. plural end in -a;

  • II group: contains male and female parisyllaba. It contains also imparisyllaba of any gender which have two consonant before the desinence is of the genitive signualr, like:

    mons, montis, m., «mountain, mount»

    Nouns of this group all end in e in the ablative singualr, in ium in the genitive plural, and if neuter in a in the nominative, accusative and vocative.

  • III group: it contains neuter nouns ending in -e, -al, -ar in the nominative singular, like

    mare, maris, n. «sea»

    tribūnal, tribuālis, n., «court, tribune, seat»;

    animal, animālis, n., «animal»;

Declension of the I group nouns

Depending on the gender, nouns belonging to the first group have different case endings:

  • Masculine nouns of the I gorup are decline as consul, consŭlis, m.,;

  • Feminine nouns as lībertas, libertātis, «freedom, liberty»;

  • Neuter nouns as corpus, corpŏris, n.

As you can notice there are equal forms:

  • The nominative and the vocative singular.

  • The nominative, accusative, and vocative plural Masculine and feminine

  • The neuter nominative, accusative and vocative, singular and plural.

  • The dative and ablative plural (all genders).

Other nouns belonging to the I group are:

labor, labōris, m.;

orator, oratōris, m., «horator»;

homo, homĭnis, m., «The man»;

sermo, sermōnis, m., «speech»;

lex, legis, f., «the law»;

virtus, virtūtis, f., «virtue»;

nomen, nomĭnis, n., «name»;

vulnus, vulnĕris, n., «the wound»
