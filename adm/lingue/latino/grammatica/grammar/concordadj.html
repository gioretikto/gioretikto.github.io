Concordance of Adjectives

Adjectives agree with their nouns in gender, number and case; as:

  • amico cenrtus, «a sure friend»

  • poëta impavidus, «the famous poet»

  • discipula sedula, «the good student girl»

Not always the substantive and adjective follow the same declension. For example, in the case of a masculine noun of the first declension like nauta, the adjective that agrees with it will be mascuiline and therefore it follows the second declension, nauta impavidus, «the intrepid sailor»; Conversely a feminine noun of the second declension, like populus alta, «a tall poplar», the adjective will be feminine and therefore of the first declension. This is how nauta impavidus and populus alta are declined:

Case Singular Plural
NOMINATIVE nauta impavidus, the intrepid sailor nautae impavidī, the intrepid sailors
GENITIVE nautae impavidī, of the intrepid sailor nautārum impavidōrum, of the intrepid sailors
DATIVE nautae impavido, to the intrepid sailor nautis impavidīs, to the intrepid sailors
ACCUSATIVE nautam impavidum, the intrepid sailor nautas impavidos, the intrepid sailors
VOCATIVE nauta impavide, oh intrepid sailor! nautae impavidī, oh intrepid sailors
ABLATIVE nauta impavido, with the intrepid sailor nautis impavidīs, with the intrepid sailors

Case Singular Plural
NOMINATIVE populus alta, the tall poplar populi altae, the tall poplars
GENITIVE populi altae, of the tall poplar populorum altarum, of the tall poplars
DATIVE populo altae, to the tall poplar populis talls, to the tall poplars
ACCUSATIVE populum altam, the tall poplar populos altas, the tall poplars
VOCATIVE popule alta, oh tall poplar populi altae, oh tall poplars
ABLATIVE populo alta, with the tall poplar populis talls, with the tall poplars
