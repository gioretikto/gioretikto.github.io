Adjectives of three terminations

Third declension adjectives that possess three nominative singular endings, one ending for each gender are declined like acer (m.), acris (f.), acre (n.):

The Adjectives of three terminations are in total 13, they are:

acer, acris, acre - sharp, sour, bitter

alăcer, alăcris, alăcre, àlacre - Lively, brisk, quick, eager

celĕber, celĕbris, celĕbre frequented, famous

celer, celĕris, celĕre swift, fleet

puter, putris, rotten, decaying

salūber, salūbris, salūbre healthy, salubrious , salutary

volŭcer, volŭcris, volŭcre - flying, winged

campester, campestris, campestre campestre

equester, equestris, equestre of or pertaining to an equestrian/cavalry

palūster, palūstris, palūstre swampy, marshy

pedester, pedestris, pedestre, pedestrian

silvester, silvestris, silvestre - wooded, wild

terrester, terrestris, terrestre terrestrial

In Latin, the names of months are used as adjectives; those ending in -ber like September, October, November, December are declined as acer. In the Classical period, these adjectives were used substantively, with the noun mensis (month), as in: mensis September. In the singular they are considered masculine while in the plural they are used in union with the noun Kalendae (1st day of each month) e.g. Kalendis Novembribus, (1st day of november), Nonae, (the 5th day of a month) and Idus (the thirteenth day of the month).
