Pronominal Adjectives

There is a group of first/second declension f adjectives, that like many pronouns, end in -ius in the nominative singular and in -ī, in the dative singular; For this reason they are called pronominal adjective. They are:

  • unus, una, unum, «one»
  • alter, altera, alterum, «the one (of two,) the other, another, the second»
  • solus, sola, solum, «alone, sole»
  • nullus, nulla, nullum, «no, none, not any»
  • totus, tota, totum, «all, whole»
  • neuter, neutra, neutrum, «neither»
  • alius, alia, aliud, «other (between many), another»
  • uter?, utra?, utrum?, «which(of two)?»
