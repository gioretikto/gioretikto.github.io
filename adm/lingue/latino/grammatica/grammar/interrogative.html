Interrogative Pronouns and Adjective

The latin interrogatives are:

quis? what man? who?, quid? what? which? (pronoun, without the feminine)

qui?, quae?, quod? who? which? (adjective of quis)

uter?, utra?, utrum?, which of two?

There are also the compunds forms quisquam, quidam? who then, what then?

The interrogative pronoun has the same forms as the relative pronoun, except for the masculine and fenimine nominative singular quis and the neuter nominative and accusative singular quid.

Quis?, quid?, «Who?», «What?» are only used as pronouns to ask for the identity of a person or thing. The form quis is used either for the masculine or feminine.

  • Quis es? «Who are you?»

  • Quid agis? «What Do you do?»

  • Quis hoc fecit? «Who has done this?»

  • Quis est dux? «Who is the leader?».

  • Quis est vir? «Who is the man?»

  • Quem vidisti? «Who Did you see?»

quis is an apposition when found united to a substantive:

  • Quis homo?

  • Quis philosophus hoc adfirmat? «Which pilosopher says this?».

  • Quis hospes?

homo, hospes, etc, are to be regarded as placed in apposition to the interrogative quis.

The interrogative qui?, quae?, quod?

Qui?, quae?, quod? is the adjective of quis and it is used for asking question about the quality of a person or thing while quis is used to ask questions about the identity; it is declined as the relative pronoun. As in the case of the relative pronoun, the interrogative uses also the forms quocum?, quacum?, quibuscum?, instead of cum quo?, cum qua?, cum quibus?.

  • At qui dux est? Optimus omnium. - «What kind of leader is he? The best of all»

  • Qui vir laudat Dominum? - «Which man is praising The Lord? The best of all»

  • Quod animal cornua habet? - «What kind of leader is he? The best of all»

Compound adjectives quis?, quid? e qui?, quae?, quod?

The interrogative adjectives and adverbs frequently are made more emphatic by the enclitic indeclinable particles like -nam, num -ec. The compounds quisnam and quinam have the signification and declension of quis and qui respectively. Ecquis and numquis are compounded from the indefinite particle -ec and the interrogative -num respectively. They are declined and used like quid

MASCHILE FEMMINILE NEUTRO TYPE Meaning
quisnam? - quidnam? Emphatic pron. Who indeed? what indeed?
quinam? quaenam? quodnam? Emphatic adj. Which indeed?
ecquis? - ecquid? pron. Is there anyone?, anything?, someone?, something?
ecqui? ecquae? ecquod? adj. Is there any?
numquis? - numquid? pron. Is there anyone?, anything?, someone?, something?
numqui? numquae? numquod? adj. Is there any?

The interrogative uter?, utra?, utrum?

When the question is "which of two?" is uter?, utra?, utrum?. Uter is decline as a first/second class adjective with the charateristics of the pronomimal declension i.e with gen. sing. in -īus and dat. in -i:
