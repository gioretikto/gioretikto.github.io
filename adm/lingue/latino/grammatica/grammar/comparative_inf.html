The Comparison of inferiority

The comparison of inferiority is used when the first term of the comparison is of a lesser degree than the second term. The comprative of minority is formed in Enlgish with the construction “less + adjective + than”; in Latin, is formed in a similar fashion by using the adverb minus (less): minus + adjective + quam:

  • Pecus est minus velox quam canis - «The sheep is slower than the dog».

  • Brutus minus doctus quam Marcus est - «Brutus is less learned hat Marcus»

  • Minus sapiens quam doctus - «Less wise than learned»

  • Tu es minus habilis quam Marcus - «You are less ables than Marcus»

  • Alcibiades timebatur non minus quam diligebatur (Nep.) - «Alcibaldes was not less feared than loved»

The Absolute Comparative

There is also another form of comparason called Absolute Comparative; The term absolute means independent, the etimology comes from the verb absolvo, "untie, loosen", because in this construction there is no second term of comparison. This comparative denotes an increased degree of the positive adjective. It can be translated with expressions like "fairly", "rather":

  • Hostes acrius pugnabant - «The enemy was foughting fairly violently»

  • Senectus est natura loquacior (Cic.) - «Old age is naturally rather talkative»

  • liberius vivebat - «He lived too freely»
