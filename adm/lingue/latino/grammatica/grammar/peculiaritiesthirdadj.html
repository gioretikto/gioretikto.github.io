Peculiarities of thirds declension adjectives

The following adjectives have the ablative singular in -e and the genitive plural in -um:

Adjective Meaning Ablative Singular Genitive plural
compos, compŏtis master compŏt-e compŏt-um
dives, divĭtis rich divĭt-e divĭt-um
mortalis, mortale mortal mortal-e mortal-um
nobilis, nobile noble nobil-e nobl-um
partĭceps, particĭpis partecipe particĭp-e particĭp-um
pauper, paupĕris poor paupĕr-e paupĕr-um
princeps, princĭpis first, foremost princĭp-e princĭp-um
sapiens, sapientis wise sapient-e sapient-um
sospes, sospĭtis safe and sound sospĭt-e sospĭt-um
superstes, superstĭtis survivor superstĭt-e superstĭt-um
vetus, vetĕris old, aged vetĕr-e vetĕr-um

The adjectives dives and vetus have peculiar declensions, with contracted forms.

Some adjectives are regular in the singular having the ablative in -i, but irregular in the genitive plural , ending in -um.

Adjective Meaning Ablative Singular Genitive plural
memor, memŏris mindful, remembering memŏr-i memŏr-um
immĕmor, immemŏris forgetful immemŏr-i immemŏr-um
inops, inŏpis Lacking wealth, poor inŏp-i inŏp-um
supplex, supplĭcis kneeling, begging supplĭc-i supplĭc-um
vigil, vigĭlis vigilant vigĭl-i vigĭl-um

Supplex and vigil used as nouns have the ablative singular ending in -e:

adulatus a supplice - praised by the begger.

Some adjectives are invariable, and have the same-case endings throughout the declension, whatever the gender and number:

  • frugi, "frugal", "virtuous";

  • nequam, "worthless, good-for-nothing";

  • tot, "so many";

  • quot, "how many; as many";

  • aliquot, "some; several; a few"
