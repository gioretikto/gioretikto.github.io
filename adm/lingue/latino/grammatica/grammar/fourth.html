The Fourth Declension

Nouns of the fourth declension can be masculine, feminine and neuter and are distinguished by the ending of the genitive singualr in -us. The thematic vowel of the third declension is u. Masculine and feminine nouns also end in -us in the nominative singular while neuter nouns in u. Masculine and Feminine nouns are decline as cantus, -us, m., «song, chant», neuter nouns are decline as cornu, -us, n., «cornel, dogwood»:

Other Nouns of the Fourth Declension are:

  • fructus, fructus, m., «fruit»;

  • motus, motus, m., «motion;

  • fletus, fletus, m., «weeping, tears»;

  • manus, manus, f., «hand»;

  • anus, anus, f., «old woman»;

  • passus, passūs, m., «step»;

  • genu, genus, n., «knee»;

  • ictus, ictūs', m., «a blow, stroke»;
