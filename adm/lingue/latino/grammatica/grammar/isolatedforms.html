Isolated forms

Certain verbs retain only few isolated forms mostly employed in greeting or discursive formulas:

  • Quaeso, "I pray" and its plural quaesŭmus "We pray", (are archaic forms of quaero and quaerĭmus) are used as adverbs broadly corresponding to the English courtesy form «please», «pray», «I beseech you, for Heaven's sake», often followed by the imperative:

    • Dic mihi, quaeso, unde venias. «Tell me, please, whence you are coming»

  • Ave, avēto (sing.), avēte (plur.; also have, havēto, havēte) are imperative forms of the disused salveo, aveo, signify ' hail,', "be greeted", «Good Morning!», «farewell!», etc.; to be noticed the form avēre iubeo, used for greeting in letters:

    • Paulus avēre iubet - «Paulus sends you his greetings»

  • Salve, salvēto (sing.), salvēte (plur.) are imperative forms of the disused salveo («I am well, healthy.»), employed as greeting formulas signify «hail!», «welcome!», «farewell»!:

    • Iubeo te salvere, «I bid you, hail»

  • Vale, valēto (sing.), valēte (plur.) are the imperatives froms of valeo («I am well»), used as greetings and farewell formulas, particularly in letters; signify: «sta’ bene!», «state bene!», «addio!». Si possono trovare anche più forme isolate accostate, come nell’espressione: Vale atque salve. «Stammi bene e addio!».

    • Cedo (sing.), cette (plur.) are arcaico imperative forms derived from ce + dăre expressing a double meaning: «da’ qua», «dammi qua», «date qua», or: «di’», «dimmi», «ditemi». Es.: Cedo dextram! «Dammi la mano (destra)!», «Qua la mano!». Cette dexteras! «Qua la mano!», «Date la mano!». Cedo istuc tuum consilium. (Ter.) «Dimmi codesto tuo piano».
