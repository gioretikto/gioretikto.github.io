The parts of speech in Latin

Words are divided into nine Parts of Speech; the major difference with english is the absence of the determinative article. Five are inflected parts:

  • Nouns

  • Adjectives

  • Participles

  • Pronouns

  • Verbs

Four are invariable:

  • Adverbs

  • Prepositions

  • Conjunctions

  • Interjections

Nouns

A Noun is a word used to express the name of a person, place, thing, or idea. Like :

  • Caesar (Caesar)

  • Rex (The king)

  • Puer (Teh boy)

  • insula (island)

  • Puella (The girl)

  • Italia (Italy)

  • Pueritia (Boyhood, childhood, youth).

Nouns have gender:

  • Masculine, like : pater, frater, rex, dux.

  • Feminine, like : puella, mater, sor, regina.

  • Neuter: pirum, ferrum, bonu, vivere, fas

The names of rivers, winds, and months are generally masculine: Padus, m., "the Po"; Tiberis,m. The Tiber River; Ianuarius, m., «January»;

The names of plants, trees, islands and regions are generally feminine Pinus, f., «pine tree», Malus, f., «an apple tree»; Aegyptus, f., «Egypt»; Rhodus, f., «Rhodes»; Corinthus, f., «Corinth»;

Nouns have also a number: Singular or Plural and a case: Nominative, Genitive, Dative, Accusative, Ablative and Vocative.

Pronouns

A pronoun is a word used in the place of a noun, some examples are:

is, he

tu, you (sing.)

ea, she

ego, I

id, it

vos, you (pl.)

quī, who

nōs, we

Nouns and pronouns are often called Substantives.

Verbs

A verb from the Latin verbum meaning word, is the principal part of the speech, and it is used to express action, assert something or determine the state of being:

sum, I am

voco, I call

amant, they love

Adverbs

An adverb is a word used to describe a verb, an adjective, or another adverb, answering questions such as how?, in what way?, when?, where?, and to what extent?. The English word adverb derives from Latin adverbium, coumpounded of ad- ("to"), and verbum ("word", "verb"), literally 'joined to a verb.

cras, tomorrow.

hodiē, today.

Nunc, now.

magis, more

minus, less

parum, not much

Preposition

Preposition are a class of words used to express spatial or temporal relations (in, under, towards, before) or mark various semantic roles (of, for). The word preposition comes from Latin: prae ("before") and Latin: ponere ("to put"). This refers to the situation in Latin and Greek (and in English), where prepositions are placed before their complement, and are hence "pre-positioned".

corona ex auro, a crown made of gold.

quam ob causam, for this reason

in illo tempore, in that particular circumstance.

Sumus in urbe, we are in town

in silva, in the forest.

Conjunctions

A conjunction is a connecting word used to join words, phrases, clauses or sentences. A conjunction can connect equal ideas (coordinate):

et, atque, ac, and

pater et mater, the father and the mother

Rex aequus ac sapiens., A king just and wise.

Odi et amo, I hate and love.

A conjunction can also be anthitetic:

sed, verum, autem

action disgraceful not merely to see, but even to hear of!

The antithetic particle is sometimes omitted, in which case the first word of the second clause receives a strong emphasis:

Dum lacrimat, Troia ardet, While she weeps, Troy burns.

Interjections

These are words showing strong feeling or emotion:

O vir fortis!, What a brave man.

Heu me miserum!, Poor me.

heus, halloo!

ō, oh!
