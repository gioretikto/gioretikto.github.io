Genitive case

The main function of the genitive case is to express ownership and show possesion, answering the question кoгo? (of whom?), чeгo? /chego/ "of what?". The owner of something is put in the genitive case:

  • Teтaдb Ивaн на стoлe /Tetrad' Ivana na stole/ Ivan's notebook is on the table

The owner of the notebook is Iva whis is put in the genitive case, wheread the word на стoлe is put in the prepositional case, since it is denoting a location. Please note that, despite the flexible word in Russian sentences, a sentence in which a possessor is put before the object doesn't sound natural.

The words put in the genitive are underlined

  • Это машина Игоря? /Eto mashina Igorya?/ ”Is this Igor's car?”

  • Сестра Алины читает газету. /Sestra Aliny chitayet gazetu/ Alina's sister is reading а newspaper

  • Это кот Антона. /Eto kot Antona/ ”This is Anton's cat. ”

  • Брат Ивана гуляет впарке. /Brat Ivana gulyayet v parke/ ”Ivan's brother is walking in the park.”

  • Комната мамы очень большая. /komnata тату ochen' bol'shaya./ ”Mom's room is very big.”

The genitive case is used form the expression "I have" in Russian.

Negation

The genitive case is always used after negated forms for the object or person that is absent

  • У меня нет машины, у меня есть велосипед. (U mеnуа net mashiny, u mепуа yest' velosiped. (I don't have а саг. I have а bicycle)

  • У вас есть мелкие деньги? /U vas yest' meIkiye den 'gi?/ Dou have small change?”

  • У него нет сестры. /U пеgо net sestry/ He doesn't have а sister

  • Вчера не было дождя. /Vchera nе bylo dozhdya/ ”Yesterday, there was nо rain.”

  • У меня нет времени. /U тепуа net vremeni/ I have по time
