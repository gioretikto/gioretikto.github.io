Present Tense

Thus one Russian present tense corresponds to several English present tenses. Compare

In Russian я читаю (Ya chitaiu) correspoonds to English

  • I read

  • I am reading. Я читаю книгу. (Ya chitaiu knigu)

  • I have been reading

Thus, phrases like “I do” and “I’m doing” are translated into Russian the same way: “Я делаю” (Ya delaiu).

  • Я работаю. - I work /I've been working/ I'm working

Other examples

  1. Я иду на работу. (Ya idu na rabotu.) I go to work

  2. Я читаю каждый день. I read every day.

  3. Что ты сейчас делаешь? What are you doing now?

  4. Я читаю. I'm reading

  5. Я давно читаю. I've been reading for a long time.

  6. Где ты живёшь? Where do you live?

  7. Я живу в Москве. I live in Moscow.

When to use present tense

The Russian present tense is used to express:

  1. Actions which are happening at the time of speaking

    • Она едет на работу. - (She is driving to work.)

  2. Actions which occur regularly

    • Земля вращается вокруг солнца. -(The earth revolves around the sun.)

  3. Habitual actions

    • Каждое утро я встаю в 8 часов. - (I get up at 8 o'clock every morning.)

  4. Actions which began in the past and are still happening or just stopped

    • Я живу в Москве уже целый год. - (I have been living in Moscow for a whole year.)

Verb Conjugation

A Russian verb has six forms in the present tense, one for each of the subject pronouns (1st, 2nd and 3rd persons, singular and plural). By looking at the ending of the verb we can tell the person and the number of the verb. This is called verb conjugation.

The Present tense form consists of a verb stem followed by an ending denoting person and number. Most Russian verbs fall into two groups: first conjugation and second conjugation.

  1. First conjugation verbs take the endings -ю(-у ); -ешь; -ет; -ем; -ете and -ют (-ут) in the Present Tense.

  2. Second conjugation verbs take the endings -ю (-у); -ишь; -ит; -им; -ите and -ят (-ат) in the Present Tense.
