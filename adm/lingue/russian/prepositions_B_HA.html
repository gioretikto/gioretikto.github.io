Location. Prepositions в (во) and на. В доме vs. дома

As we learned, there are six cases in Russian. Let’s take a closer look at the prepositional case, which indicates the place or position and is always used with prepositions. This lesson teaches how to form the prepositional when answering где, /gdie/ "where" questions, then walks through the difference between the prepositions "in" and "on". It also explains a few exceptions to the grammar rules for prepositioinal noun endings.

The prepositions в (во- before consonant в or ф) - "in, inside" and на - "on, on the surface of" followed by nouns in the prepositional case are used to indicate location. For Example: в сумке - in the bag; в комнате - in the room; на столе - on the table; на стене - on the wall. Study the following examples. Click each statement to listen, then say it.

  1. Мой учебник в сумке. My textbook is in the bag.

  2. B самолёте - On the plane

  3. Мой друг сейчас во Франции, в Париже. My friend is in France, in Paris now.

  4. Моя сестра сейчас в России, в Москве. My friend is in Russia, in Moscow now.

  5. Твоя ручка на столе.- Your pen in on the table.

Notes

  • The preposition в becomes во before a word starting in two consonants with the first в or ф, for example: во Владивостоке, во Франции (во is always unstressed, therefore it pronounced as [ва]).

  • Because of the consonant cluster rule в is pronounced as [ф] before voiceless consonants. Compare: в сумке [ф сумк']; в Париже [ф париж] and в России [в рас'и], в Москве [в маскв'е].

Location: В or НА?

Both prepositions в and на are used to indicate location in place of English "in", "at" and "on".

The preposition в is used:

  1. with most places, buildings and certain parts of buildings, for example:

    • в университете - (in, at) university

    • в аудитории - (in, at) lecture-room

    • в школе - (in, at) school

    • в театре - (in, at) theatre

    • в офисе - (in, at) office

    • в комнате - (in, at) room

    • в классе - (in) class

  2. with cities, towns and countries, for example:

    • в городе - (in) city

    • в деревне - (in) village

    • в стране - (in) country/state

    • в Москве - (in) Moscow

    • в Канаде - (in) Canada

    • в Росси и - (in) Russia

The preposition на is used:

  1. with the names of a few places that must be memorized:

    • на заводе - (in, at) factory

    • на даче - (in, at) cottage out of town

    • на вокзале - (in, at) railway station

    • на ста нции - (in, at) station

    • на стадионе - (in, at) stadium

    • на ку хне - (in, at) kitchen

    • на кафере - (in, at) department

    • на факультете - (in, at) faculty

    • на курсе - in a year of university

  2. with the names of events, activities, for example:

    • на лекции - (at) lecture

    • на балете - (at) ballet

    • на собрании - (at) meeting

    • на уроке - (at) lesson

    • на концерте - (at) concert

    • на рабо те - (at) work

  3. with certain parts of city that must be memorized:

    • на улице - (in, at) street

    • на площади - (in, at) square

    • на проспе кте - (in, at) broad street

  4. with names of islands and peninsulas, and with points on the compass:

    • на острове - (on) island

    • на Аляске - in Alaska

    • на севере - in the North

    • на юге - in the South

    • на западе - in the West

    • на восто ке - in the East

В доме vs дома

Learn the difference between two expressions:

в доме - means "in a building/house"

дома - means "at home"
