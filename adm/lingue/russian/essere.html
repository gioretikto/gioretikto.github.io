Verbo essere

Russians omit the verb "to be" (быть) [byt’], in the present tense. Examples:

  • Я учитель. - I am a tutor. - Literally: I -- tutor.

  • Я студе́нт - I am a student

  • Ты учи́тель. – You are a teacher. [ty u-cheé-teel’]

  • Он до́ктор. – He is a doctor. [on dók-tar]

  • Она́ краси́вая. – She is beautiful. [a-ná kra-sée-va-ya]

  • Вы до́брые. – You are kind (plural). [vy dób-ry-ye]

  • Они́ у́мные. – They are smart. [a-née úm-ny-ye]

  • Мне холодно. - I am cold. - Literally: Cold to me.

At the same time, "to be" is used in the future and past tense:

  • Я буду читать книгу. - I will read the book.

  • Я был в театре. - I was at the theatre.
