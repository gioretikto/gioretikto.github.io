Cr3+ ions are characterized by a strong tendency to form complexed, of which thousands are known, and in which Cr is nearly always six-coordinate (octahedral symmetry). By far the most common interaction of chromium with proteins is that in commercial leather tanning (concia). Cr (III) is used for stabilizing the leather by crosslinking collagen fibers. As a final product, Cr tanned leather contains 4 to 5% of Cr, which is tightly bound to the proteins.

The most commonly used tanning process involves the application of a tanning liquor made drom basic chromium sulfate. This base chromium sulfate liquor is produced in a variety of pH values by the reduction of sodium dicrhomate with different amounts of sulfuric acid: the resulting product containing a varying relative fraction of hydroxyl groups linked to chromium e.g. Cr2(SO4)2, Cr(OH)(SO4), Cr2(OH)4 (SO4), Cr(OH)2.

Cr(VI) non viene intenzionalment eutilizzato nel processo conciario, Cr(VI) si può formare a seguito dell’ossidazione del Cr(III) impiegato per la conciae/o riconcia delle pelli.
