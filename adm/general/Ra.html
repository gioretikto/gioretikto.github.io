Radium

Pure radium is a volatile silvery white metal which becomes black when exposed to air. The Curies discovered that natural radium-226 was the most stable form of naturally occuring radium, with a physical half-life of 1600 years, yet it is millions of times more radioactive than uranium. Marie Curie came up with the word 'radioactive' to describe the property of the elements she discovered.
