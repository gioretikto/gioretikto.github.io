Chocolate

Chocolate is made up of a lot of different components. The main ingredients are cocoa butter which stores the heat when chocolate is warmed and cocoa powder which gives the chocolate its magnificent taste.

Cocoa butter as is known is made of little crystals. In total we differ between 6 different crystals of which there are different forms of stability. The more stable crystals give the chocolate a better taste and therefore much higher quality.

Table of various crystals in cocoa butter. Unstable crystals melt at a lower temperature than stable crystals which normally melt at body temperature and give the “melt-on-the-tongue taste”.
Form Stability Melting Point °C Basic Polymorph
I unstable 17 γ/sub-α
II meta-stable 23 Α
III meta-stable 25 β'
IV meta-stable 28 β'
V stable 33 β
VI most stable 36 β

Storing chocolate at low temperatures (such as those in convenience store refrigerators) results in a phase change of the chocolate from the desired beta phase to a less dense mix of alpha and beta phases (or even just alpha phase).
