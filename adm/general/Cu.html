Copper

Copper has two stable oxidation states +1 and +2. Consequently, red cuprous oxide (Cu2O) and black cupric oxide CuO, are considered the stable fors of copper oxide.

Cuprix oxide can be obtained easily by heating cuprous oxide Cu2O or copper in air at 1273-1373 K.

Cu2O + 1/2O ⟶ 2CuO

or


2Cu + 2O2 ⟶ 2CuO

In the industrial method, it is prepated by heating malachite ore, according to the reaction below

CuCO3 ⋅ Cu(OH)2 ⟶ 2CuO + CO2 + H2O + O2

Copper sulfate (Cu2SO4)

Copper sulfate (Cu2SO4) is a white-colored powder, butt forms hydrates CuSO4·nH2O, where n can range from 1 to 7. The pentahydrate (n = 5), (CuSO4⋅5H2O), is an azurite blue crystal. Copper sulfate is being used as an electrolytic solution, pigment, insecticide, antiseptic, mordant, battery material, medicine and so on.
