Amphoteric ions

Species that have the properties of acids and bases are called Amphoteric, from the gree ἀμφότερος, meaning "both". We saw that water ionized reversibly to provide both protons and hydroxide ions. This makes water both an acid and a base by Arrhenius definition. Suppose we have a generic anion HA−, its reaction as a base with water is described by the equilibrium

HA− + H2O ⇌ H2A + OH−

regulated by the constant Kb, which is related with Ka,H2A via the relation Ka x Kb = Kw which we derived in the last section.

Kb = [HA][OH−]/[A−] = Kw/KHA

Its reaction as acid with water is described by the equilibrium

HA− + H2O ⇌ A− + H3O+

Regulated by Ka,HA− which corresponds to the second ioniziation constant of H2A, Ka,H2A. You can estimate the pH of a slution of an amphoteric species by comparing Ka with Kb.

The hydogen carbonate, HCO3− ion is typical of amphoteric species. The acid behavior of the hydrogen carbonate ion is described by the second acid ionization constant, Ka2 of carbonic acid. The behavior as a base of HCO3− is described by Kb. From the relation Ka x Kb = Kw derived in the last section, we can calculate it from the first ionization constant Ka,1.

In the case of the hydrogen carbonate, its acid equlibrium is

HCO3− + H2O ⇌ H3O+ + CO32−

The expression for the equilibrium constant is

Ka = [H3O+][CO32−]/[HCO3−]

Ka = Ka,2 = 4.7 x 10−11, the second ionization constant for carbonic acid.

The equlibrium for the carbonate as a base is

HCO3− + H2O ⇌ OH− + H2CO3

Regulated by the equlibrium constant Kb = Kw/Ka,1 = 2.2 x 10−8.

Example 1. Amphotheric Acid-Base systems. Write the equilibria of 0.20 M sodium hydrogen sulfite, and determine whether its pH is grreater or less than 7, knowing the that Hydrogen sulfite ion has a Ka = 6.3 x 10−8.

Strategy. First, write the chemical reactions in which the amphoteric specis acts as an acid, and the reaction in which it acts as a base. Next, write the expression for the equlibrium constant for each reaction, Ka and Kb. Determine the numberical values of the two constants to understand which equlibrium dominates.

Solution. Sodium hydrogen sulfite dissociates into sodium ions and the amphoteric hydrogen sulfite ion.

NaHSO3 ⟶ Na+ + HSO3−

The following table illustrates the amphoteric nature of the hydrogen sulfite ion. The soluition is acidic because the HSO3− ion is a stronger acid than it is a base.

Amphoteric Reactions of the Hydrogen Sulfite ion
Hydrogen sulfite as an Acids Hydrogen sulfite as a Base
HSO3− + H2O ⇌ H3O+ + SO32− HSO3− + H2O ⇌ OH− + H2SO3
Ka = Ka,2 = 6.3 x 10−8 Kb = Kw / Ka,1 = 7.1 x 10−13

Example 2. Calculate the pH of the following aqueous solutions 0.3 M of the following salts: a) KClO4 b) NaNO3.

a) potassium perchlorate is completely dissociated in K+ and ClO4−, which do not react with H2O being respectively the conjugate acid and base of KOH and HClO4, strong base and acid. Hence pH = 7, independently from the salt's concentration.

b) Sodium nitrate is completely dissociated in Na+ and NO3−: which do not react with H2O being respectively the conjugate acid and base of NaOH and HNO3, strong base and strong acid. Hence pH = 7, independently from their concentrations.

Example 3. Calculate the pH of a solution 0.20 M of sodium formiate, HCOONa knowing the acidic constant for the formiate ion is Ka = 5.5 x 10−11.

Since Kb = 10−14/ 1.8 x 10−4 = 0.5 x 10−10, we know that the nature of the formiate ion in water is basic, reacting as follows

[HCOO−] + H2O ⇌ HCOOH + OH−

Kb = Kw/Ka = [HCOOH][OH−]/[HCOO−] = [OH]2]/[HCOO−]

by letting [OH−] = x, we have

Kw/Ka = [HCOOH][OH−]/[HCOO−] = x2/(0.20 − x) ≈ x2 / 0.20

[OH−] = x = (0.20 ⋅ 10−14/2.1 ⋅ 10−14)1/2 = 3.1 ⋅ 10−6 M

pOH = 5.5 and pH = 8.5

L'anfotersimo di una sostanza generica MOH è spiegabile nel seguente modo: un comportamento acido prevede la rottura del legame O-H con formazione degli ioni MO- ed H+. Al contrario, un comportamento basico implica la rottura del legame M-OH con formazione di M+ ed OH-. Il verificarsi dell'uno o dell'altro caso dipende dall'energia dei legami M-O ed O-H, a sua volta legata direttamente alle caratteristiche chimiche di X come la sua elettronegatività. Nel caso in cui le energie di M−O and O−H are similar, we have the phenomen of amphoterism.
