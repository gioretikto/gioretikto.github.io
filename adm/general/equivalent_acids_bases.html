Equivalents of acids, bases and salts

We've already introduced the concept of equivalent weight or mass in chapter 1. If we want to calculate the equivalent weight of acids and bases we can apply the general definition. For acids and bases we notice that 1 gramm of hydrogen corresponds to one mole of protons, since

H+ + e− ⟶ H

1 g		 1 mol 1 mol
1 g

We can thus give the following definitions:

The equivalent weight of an acid is the quantity in grams of acid that can donate 1 mole of protons to a base
The equivalent weight of a base is the quantity in grams of base that can accept 1 mole of protons from an acid

It follows that the equivalent weight of HCl is equal to its mole since it liberates only one proton to a base. On the other hand H3PO4, can have three equivalent weights, one equal to its mole, another to a half-mole, and another to 1/3 of its mole, depeding on the number of protons involved in the acid-base reaction.
