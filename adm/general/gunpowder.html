Gunpowder

The black gunpowder ingridients have remained about the same since its Chinese invention. It is a mixture of saltpete (potassium nitrate, KNO3), sulfur S and charcoal C. A fuse ignites this, and when the temperature becomes high enough chemical reactions start. The main reaction can be represented by

4KNO3− + 5C ⟶ 2N2 + 5CO2 + 2K2O

and as well the oxidation of sulfur to sulfur dioxide SO2 and sulfate (SO42−) takes place

4KNO3− + 5S ⟶ 2N2 + 5SO2 + 2K2O

The chemical reaction involved produces a lot of energy and gases. Any chemical reactoin would become faster at higher temperatures. Therefore, as a heat-producing reaction proceeds, the temperature goes up and hence the reaction becomes even faster. The other factor is the production of gas that creates the force of the explosion. The sudden expansion of gas creates a detonating sound (boom sound).
