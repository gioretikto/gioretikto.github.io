Acid–Base Equilibria

An acid reacts with water to produce hydronium ion (hydrogen ion) and the conjugate base ion. The process is called acid ionization or acid dissociation. For a strong acid, which ionizes completely in solution, the concentrations of ions are determined by the stoichiometry of the reaction from the initial concentration of acid.

HA (aq) + H2O (l) ⟶ H3O+ (aq) + A− (aq)

However, for a weak acid such as acetic acid, the concentrations of ions in solution are determined from the acid-ionization constant (also called the acid-dissociation constant), which is the equilibrium constant for the ionization of a weak acid.
To find the acid-ionization constant, write HA for the general formula of a weak, monoprotic acid. The acid-ionization equilibrium in aqueous solution is

HA (aq) + H2O (l) ⇌ H3O+ (aq) + A− (aq)

The corresponding equilibrium constant is

K c = [ H 3 O + ] [ A − ] [ HA ] H 2 O ]

Assuming that this is a dilute solution and that the reaction occurs to only a small extent, the concentration of water will be nearly constant. Rearranging this equation gives

K a = [ H 2 O ] K c = [ H 3 O + ] [ A − ] [ HA ]

Thus, Ka, the acid-ionization constant, equals the constant [H2O]Kc.

As an example consider an aqueous solution of the weak acid acetic acid, CH3COOH, the sour constituent of vinegar. When the acid is added to water, it reacts according to the equation

CH3COOH (aq) + H2O (l) ⇌ H3O+ (aq) + CH3COO− (aq)

Because acetic acid is a weak electrolyte, the acid ionizes to a small extent in water (about 1% or less, depending on the concentration of acid).

Logarithmic Scale of Relative Acid Strength, pKa

The values of the acid dissociation constant cover many orders of magnitude, so many chemists use a logarithmic scale to report and compare relative acid strengths.

pKa = −log10 Ka

For example, the value of Ka for ethanoic acid in water at 298 K is 1.78 x 10-5.

pKa = −log10 (1.78 x 10-5) = 4.75

The pKa value becomes smaller as the acid strength increases.

From the pKa = 4.76 of acetic acid, we deduce it is a weak acid.

CH3COOH + H2O ⥄ CH3COO− + H3O+   Ka = [CH3COO−] [H2O] / [CH3COOH ] = 1.74 x 10−5;   pKa = 4.76

Instead of water we can consider different species, say ammonia. Ammonia gets protonated generating the ammonium ion, NH4+, having pKa = 9.24 thus a weaker acid than acetic acid. Ammonia will have thus the nature of base in the following equilibrium:

CH3COOH + NH3 ⥂ CH3COO− + NH4+
pKa = 4.76 pKa = 9.24
