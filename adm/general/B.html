Boron

B is a typical metalloid having properties intermediate between metals and electronagetive non-metals. B has a very high ionization potential. Thus, it forms covalent complexes but not B3+ ionic complexes. BX3 compounds have an incomplete octet and act as acceptors (Lewis acids), with B sp3 hybridization twords many Lewis basis such as amine and phosphines. Several oxyacids are known: Metaboric acids, (HBO2)n, and the metaborate ion do not exist as monomers. Orthoboric acid, also known as boric acid (H3BO3), exists as a hydrogen-bonded layered structure, which explains the flaky form in which it is available. Orthoboric acid is formed when boron halindes are hydrolised or when dilute HCl is added to a solution of borax

B4O72− + 2H2O ⟶ B(OH)4− + H3O+

It is obtained as a white solid (m.p. 170 °C) on subsequent crystallization.
It is a weak acid (Ka = 10−9). However it differs from other protonic acids. It does not act as a proton donor but as a Lewis acid by accepting the hydroxyl ion, completing its octet:

(OH)3B + 2H2O ⇌ H3O+ + [B(OH)4]−

When heated, orthoboric acid first forms metaboric acid (empirical formula HBO2) and then boron trioxide.
The structure of orthoboric acid is based on the planr B(OH)3 unit, individual units being joined together by H-bonds linking the O of different B(OH)3 groups are unsymettrical, i.e. they are not midway between the O atoms. The layers of 2D sheets are held together by weak van der Waals forces, with a relatively wide distance of 0.318 nm, and this accounts for the clevage of the solid into flakes.

Boric acid is another commonly used solid lubricant. The layer crystal structure of H3BO3 enables its good lubrication capabilities.

Boron trioxide, B2O3

L'impiego principale dell'acido borico, è, però, come materia prima per la produzione dell'anidride, l'ossido di boro, B2O3. Fondendo (a 450 °C) in un liquido capace di disciogliere molti ossidi metallici, l'ossido di boro (spesso sotto forma di acido) si adopera come flussante, un materiale, cioè, che pulisce i metalli preliminarmente alla saldatura. L'ossido di boro si adopera pure per fabbricare le fibre di vetro e il vetro al borosilicato, un vetro a bassissimo coefficiente di dilatazione termina, come il pirex.
