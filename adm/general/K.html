Potassium

Potassium-40 (40K) is a radioactive isotope of potassium which has a long half-life of 1.251 × 109 years. It makes up 0.012% (120 ppm) of the total amount of potassium found in nature, (39K is 93.1% and 41 K is 6.88%, both are stable.). Potassium is common in our bodies, making up roughly 0,2% of the body's total weight. We get it from eating different foods that contain 40K, such as bananas and avocados. This means that a typical human body contains about 120 g of K of which 0.144 g are radiocative potassium-40.The potassium-40 nucleus is unstable because the 19 protons are overwhelmed by the 21 neutruons. So, a neutron changes itself into a proton, to make calcium-40, send out a beta particle in the process.

19 40 K → 20 40 Ca + 0 -1 β

The other major source of internal radiation isc carbons, comprising about 23% of the mass of a typical human body. Most carbon atoms are C-12 but a tiny percentage is the radiocative isotope-14, which also undergoues radiocative beta decay.
