induction = (In a typical proof by induction, there is a statement Pn to be proved true for every positive integer n. The proof consists of three steps: 1. The statement is verified for n = 1. 2.) The statement is assumed true for n = k. 3.) With this assumption made, the statement is then proved to be true for n = k + 1. The assumption that is made in step 2 is called the inductive assumption or the induction hypothesis.)
