Fractions

In LaTeX, \dfrac, \frac, and \tfrac are used to create fractions, but they differ in terms of formatting:

  • \frac creates a fraction in the standard size. It adjusts its size based on the context (inline vs. display), making it suitable for general use. Example: $$ \rho_m = \frac{m_T}{\frac{4}{3} \pi R_T^3}$$ produces;

    ρ m = m T 4 3 π R T 3

  • \dfrac always creates a "display style" fraction, which is larger and more prominent, regardless of whether it‚Äôs used inline or in display math mode. It is typically used for fractions that need to stand out more. Example: $$ \rho_m = \frac{m_T}{\dfrac{4}{3} \pi R_T^3}$$ produces;

    ρ m = m T 4 3 π R T 3

  • \tfrac creates a "text style" fraction, which is smaller and more compact, making it more suitable for inline use where space is limited. Example: $$ \rho_m = \frac{m_T}{\tfrac{4}{3} \pi R_T^3}$$ produces.

    ρ m = m T 4 3 π R T 3
