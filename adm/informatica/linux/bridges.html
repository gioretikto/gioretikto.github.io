Bridges

Networks connect to other networks through repeaters, bridges or routers.

Bridges connect networks at the data link level (using the MAC address) . They can be used to connect two indentical netwrok types or to interconnect two dissimilar networks which have the same network layer. An example of the tallet is the famility of IEEE 802 LAN standards which have different media access control protocols but the same network layer. MAC addresses are used to send frames between LANs connected by bridges.
