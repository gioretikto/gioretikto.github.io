alsamixer

The alsamixer utility is an ncurses application, which means that it is viewed graphically from a shell. It can be used to manage sound levels for more than one sound card on a computer, each with multiple devices representing it. Type alsamixer from a Terminal window to start it in playback mode (to adjust audio output) or alsamixer -V capture (to select an audio capture device and adjust audio capture level).

Tracks that are muted appear with an MM at the bottom of the slider. When unmuted, 00 appears instead. Press m to unmute. If more tracks are available than can appear on the screen, the nght arrow key enables you to scroll to the right to display additional track bars.

TIP: If you have more than one sound card, each sound card is identified by a number, with zero, identifying the first sound card. For example, to start alsamixer for your second sound card, type $ alsamixer -c 1. Here are some ways to adjust your audio tracks with alsamixer:

  • Use the nght and left arrow keys to move among the different sound tracks.

  • Use the up and down arrow keys to adjust the levels of the current tracks.

  • Press m to mute or unmute the current track (playback mode only).

  • Press the spacebar to make the current track the capture device, for devices such as microphone or line-in that are appropriate for capturing audio. Then adjust the Capture bar to set the level at which audio is captured. (Note that this feature works in capture mode only: alsamixer -v capture.)
