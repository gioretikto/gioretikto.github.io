Stacking terms

In many applications, like in chemical reactions, two terms often need to be stacked (to put one above another), where the upper term is usually some texts and lower one is a symbol covering the upper term. Generally stacking is done through the \stackrel{aup}{alow}, where aup is the upper term and alow is the lower one. Some applications of this command are shown in Table 12.13, where arrow symbols are generated through fixed-length.

  • A \stackrel{a} {\rightarrow} B

  • \mathrm{2Na + D_2} \stackrel{\mathrm{heat}} {\longrightarrow} \mathrm{2NaD}

The stacking under \stackrel{}{] may look odd if the length of the term is too long or short in comparison to that of the covering symbol.
