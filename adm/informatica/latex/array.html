Array

You can present equations with several lines, using the array statement. Inside its declaration you must:

  • Define the number of columns

  • Define column alignment

  • Define column indentation

  • Indicate column separator with & symbol &

Example: {lcr} means: 3 columns with indentations respectively left, center and right

$$\left(\begin{array}{cc}
a & -b\\
b & a
\end{array}\right)$$
( a − b b a )

or

( y 1 y 2 ⋮ y m ) = A ( x 1 x 2 ⋮ x n ) 
$$\left(\begin{array}{c}
y_1 \\
y_2\\
\vdots\\
y_m
\end{array}\right) = A \left(\begin{array}{c}
x_1 \\
x_2\\
\vdots\\
x_n
\end{array}\right)$$

For systems of equations

v ¯ 1 = a 11 v 1 + a 12 v 2 + … + a 1 n v n v ¯ 2 = a 21 v 1 + a 22 v 2 + … + a 2 n v n ⋮ v ¯ n = a n 1 v 1 + a n 2 v 2 + … + a n n v n 
$$\begin{array}{c} \bar{v}_1 = a_{11} v_1 + a_{12} v_2 + \ldots + a_{1n} v_n \\
\bar{v}_2 = a_{21} v_1 + a_{22} v_2 + \ldots + a_{2n} v_n \\
\vdots \\
\bar{v}_n = a_{n1} v_1 + a_{n2} v_2 + \ldots + a_{nn} v_n \end{array}$$

Matrices

Matrices can be assembled by using the array statement, like in this example:

$$ \left| \begin{array}{cc} x_{11} & x_{12} \\ x_{21} & x_{22} \end{array} \right|$$
| x 11 x 12 x 21 x 22 |

un'alternativa è pmatrix:

\begin{equation*}
A = 
\begin{pmatrix}
1 & 2 & 3 \\
4 & 5 & 6 \\
7 & 8 & 9
\end{pmatrix}
\end{equation*}

or bmatrix

[ x ′ y ′ ] = [ cos ⁡ θ sin ⁡ θ − sin ⁡ θ cos ⁡ θ ] [ x y ] 
$$ \begin{bmatrix}x'\\y'\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}\cos \theta &\sin \theta \\-\sin \theta &\cos \theta \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}x\\y\end{bmatrix} $$

Cases

L'ambiene case compreso in amsmath fornisce una sintassi semplificata per la creazione di sistemi di equazioni. It is particularly ssed when a definition have two or more cases. Use the case statement. Notice that the spaces after the instances of if were included inside the mbox declarations.
L'ambiente case è idoneo anche per scrivere equazioni parametrice:

$$ \begin{cases} x = A\theta \,\cos \theta \\ y = A\theta \,\sin \theta\end{cases} \; \qquad \theta \in [0,+\infty) $$
{ x = A θ cos ⁡ θ y = A θ sin ⁡ θ θ ∈ [ 0 , + ∞ )

other examples, are

$$\begin{equation*}
\begin{cases}
x= x_0 + ta\\
y= y_0 + tb & t \in \mathbb{R}\\
z= z_0 + tc\\
\end{cases}
\end{equation*}$$
{ x = x 0 + t a y = y 0 + t b t ∈ R z = z 0 + t c

\begin{cases}

1 & \text{if } x \in A.\\

\end{equation*}$$
