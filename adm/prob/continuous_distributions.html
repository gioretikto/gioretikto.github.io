Continuous Probability Distributions

Gaussian Distribution

The Gaussian distribution, also known as the normal distributionz, is a type of continuous probability distribution for a real-valued random variable. The general form of its probability density function is

P ( x ) = 1 σ 2 π e − 1 2 ( x − μ σ ) 2

The parameter μ is the mean or expectation of the distribution (and also its median and mode), while the parameter σ is its standard deviation. The density function is symmetric about its mean value μ. The inflection points for this distribution occur at one standard deviation away from the mean, i.e., at μ - σ and μ + σ.

The cumulative distribution function for the Gaussian is as follows:

F X ( x ) = 1 σ 2 π ∫ − ∞ x e − 1 2 ( t − μ σ ) 2 d t
