Queen's Gambit Declined: Exchange Variation

1.d4 d5 2.c4 e6 3.Nc3 Nf6 4.cxd5

  • solves the issue of the light square bishop for black by removing the e6 pawn;

  • white plays against the bishop by taking away good squares from it;

  • the Karlsbad pawn structure is typical for the variation; white has several plans, the most common one is the minority attack.

Main line

7. Bd3 (or Qc2) restrict Black from playing Bf4.
