Fugacity

The presence of molecular interactions distinguish the real gases from ideal gases where the molccular interactions are completely absent. For a real gas Vm ≠ RT/P and hence du ≠ RT d lnP. Since the ideal gas equations are not directly applicable to real gases, we are faced with a problem. We can either sacrifice the equations or the variable. If we abandon the general equation of chemical potential then we have to use various equations of state fitting with P-V-T data. The use of such equations of state will make the treatment more complicated. So we find it easier to retain the general form of the chemical potential and to define a new variable which has the dimensions and general propertics of pressure. The new variable is called the fugacity, which is derived from the Latin fugere, to flee, and means literally “escaping tendency’. It is denoted by f. It is a corrected pressure which applies to real gases. All the effects arising due to interactions are contained in f. The chemical potential of a pure rcal gas can be expressed in a form

μ = μ° + RT ln (f/atm)

μ° is the standard chemical potential at unit fugacity. At very low pressure

limP &to; 0 (f/P) = 1

The ratio (f/P) = γ is called the fugacity coefficient. For an ideal gas f = P and the fugacity coefficient is unity. For real gases (f/P) ≠ 1, i.e. fugacity is not the actual pressure.
