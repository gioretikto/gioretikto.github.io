Wedderburn's theorem

Every ring with unit in which every nonzero element is inverbile is called a body (not necessarily commutative with respect to multiplication), so a field is a commutative body.

8.6.1 Wedderburn's theorem. Every finite body K is a field.

Proof. We shall show that the center Z(K) = {k ∈ K | kx = xk ∀x ∈ K} coincides with K.
