Group Homomorphisms

As in the case of rings, we can have a homomorphism between groups

7.5.0 Definition. Let (G, *) and (G', ⋅) two groups. A function φ: G ⟶ G' is said to be a homomorphism if

φ(a*b) = φ(a) ⋅ φ(b)   ∀a,b ∈ G

Every isomorphism is a homomorphism, but a homomorphism need not be an isomorphism.
