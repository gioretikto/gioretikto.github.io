Exercises on rings

Exercise 1. Prove that if R is a ring with unit, the unit is unique.

Proof. Suppose e and e' are two units. Then ee' = e'e = e (because e' is a unit). It is also true that ee' = e'e = e because e is also unit. Thus e = e'.

Exercise 1. Let (A,+,) an abelian group. Consider the set

AA := {f: A ⟶ A}

∀f,g ∈ AA let

(f+g)(x) := f(x) + g(x),   (f∘g)(x) := f(g(x))

Prove that (AA, +, ∘) in not a ring. Explain for which property of ring axiomes is not satisfied.

Solution. Consider the distributive properties of ring axiomes.

f∘(g+h) = f∘g + f∘h,   (f+g)∘h = f∘h + g∘h
