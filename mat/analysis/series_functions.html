Series of functions

For every x ∈ I consider the series

f1(x) + f2(x) + ... + fn(x) = ∑ni=∞ fn(x)

the series ∑ fn(x) is said convergent in the interval I, if it is convergent for every x ∈ I, that is if the sequence {sn} of partial sums

sn = f1(x) + f2(x) + ... + fn(x)

converges to a limit f(x), otherwise is called divergent.
